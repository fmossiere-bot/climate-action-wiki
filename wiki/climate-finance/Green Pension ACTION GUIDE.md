> Your pension is likely one of your biggest financial assets. UK pension funds are worth approximately £3tn, of which a staggering £88 billion is invested in fossil fuel companies (that’s £3,000 per pension holder!). 

# Money Speaks

## Fossil Fuel Investment relies on your money

In simple terms, every time a Shell or a BP wish to drill for new oil, they call up [Chase](https://www.sierraclub.org/press-releases/2023/04/banking-climate-chaos-report-world-s-biggest-banks-continue-pour-billions#:~:text=The%20report%20shows%20that%20overall,5%20fossil%20financiers%20since%202016.) (World’s worst offending bank) [1] or [Barclays](https://good-with-money.com/2023/11/27/the-worst-6-banks-for-climate-change-and-the-best/#:~:text=Barclays,bank%20in%20Europe%E2%80%9D%20by%20campaigners.) (UK’s worst offending bank) [2]and borrow the money. Cutting off the financing is the strongest message that we can send that their days are numbered. 

Therefore, the impact of taking action here can be significant. According to [Make My Money Matter](https://makemymoneymatter.co.uk/) [3], greening your pension is 21 times more effective than giving up flying, becoming a vegetarian and changing an energy provider combined (see how they worked that out [here](https://makemymoneymatter.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/Summary-of-21x-research.pdf)) [4]. 

### Project Drawdown Takeaways
#projectdrawdown 
- Every US$1,000 a person has in savings is roughly equivalent to the direct emissions generated by flying from New York to Seattle every year 
- Eleven of the largest U.S.-based banks lend around 19.4% on average – and as high as 30% – of their portfolios to carbon-intensive industries 

## Divesting Money Decreases Emissions

And if you do take that step, you’d be in [good company](https://www.commondreams.org/news/16000-divest-fossil-fuels) [5]. So far since the fossil fuel divestment movement began, over $41 trillion dollars has been divested from big oil. Shout out to Harvard, California State University and even The Church of England (to name a few) for divesting. If you have money in a pension scheme or even a school or university, church or other org, why not make it your mission to redirect that towards building a more positive future?

An interesting point for companies to consider is that currently emissions related to pensions and banking from a business are not included in scope 1,2 or 3 (basically they’re not counted). But, when you consider that if they were to be it would over double the average UK business. Industry chatter is that this will most likely change in 2026/2027. 

### Project Drawdown Takeaways

- For the average person in the U.S., personal banking may constitute a large source of indirect greenhouse gas emissions 
- Moving from a carbon-intensive bank to a climate-responsible bank could reduce the personal banking emissions of an average U.S.-based person by 76%
- Switching banks can be a powerful, relatively easy, and affordable climate action

  

# Tips

- Take your time: Yes, this the highest scoring challenge, but that’s for good reason, because the stakes are higher too. So don’t rush your decision and make sure you feel comfortable with the new provider or fund you choose. 
- Don't be afraid to ask for help: Consult a financial advisor specialising in sustainable investing for more personalised guidance (see ethical options [here](https://ethicalinvestment.org.uk/financial-adviser/) [6]).
- Start small: If you're hesitant, consider a partial transfer (if this is offered) to a clean energy fund and gradually increase it over time. You may also want to start with a fund that is lower risk to get a sense of how it performs over the next 12 months before increasing to a higher risk fund invested solely in climate solutions. 
- Knowledge is power: Stay informed about clean energy trends and the impact your investment can have. For us it’s been great to have an app where we can regularly see progress. So, further to the obvious significant emotional benefit of knowing our money is doing good rather than harm, we’re also now more engaged with our money and how it’s performing.  

# Actions

## Making the Switch: A Step-by-Step Guide

We want to preface this by saying, we’re (obviously!) not financial advisors. This guide is put together based on our own personal experiences of moving our own pensions, along with a tonne of research. Before making any big money decisions, it can be helpful to consult a financial professional. Here’s a list of ethical financial advisors to [consider](https://ethicalinvestment.org.uk/financial-adviser/). 

  

## Week1: Research and Explore

- Firstly, assess how your current pension provider stacks up from a climate point of view. Here’s a table for the [UK](https://good-with-money.com/2024/02/22/revealed-uk-pension-providers-climate-rankings/) (the Spanish green investment market is more nascent and therefore we could not find a comparison table). Understand where they rank and if they may offer more climate-friendly funds. Obviously the preference is to move within a provider if they offer legitimate greener alternatives. 
- You can usually choose to exclude damaging (fossil fuels obviously, but also weapons, tobacco, etc) or include positive (energy transition projects like solar farms or reforestation, etc) ways of investing your money. These two approaches have increasing amounts of impact, but can also mean different amounts of risk, so be sure to do your research and understand the details. 
- For example, [Carbon Collective](https://www.carboncollective.co/401k) [7] (US based) offers both the  90/10 Climate Portfolio which means 90% is invested in companies actively helping in the energy transition, with 10% invested in regular (low carbon, fossil free) stocks. There was also an option for 40/60 of the same split. It really comes down to appetite for risk. 
- If your provider doesn’t provide what you want (and you have the option to move it), explore other possible sustainable providers. 
- Compare your options by analysing available funds, considering factors like historical performance, risk profile, and their specific focus within the clean energy sector.

Here’s a few in the UK:  

- [Pension Bee](https://www.pensionbee.com/fossil-fuel-free-pension): A simplified approach to moving your pension. Their Fossil Fuel Free option is one that we chose! 
- [Virgin Money](https://uk.virginmoney.com/investments/existing-customers/climate-change-fund/): A relatively new player, but website gives a good breakdown of their offer and the returns in the last 5 years look strong. 
- [Ethex](https://www.ethex.org.uk/): [](https://www.ethex.org.uk/)Helping everyday people make ethical investments that fund extraordinary organisations. Not exclusively climate focused. 
- [NEST](https://www.nestpensions.org.uk/schemeweb/nest/investing-your-pension/fund-choices/ethical-fund.html): Offers a range of responsible investment options, including a fossil-free Future World fund.[They take more of a spread approach to reduce risk. A good reputation.](https://www.nestpensions.org.uk/schemeweb/nest/investing-your-pension/fund-choices/ethical-fund.html) 
- [Triodos](https://www.triodos-im.com/): Specializes in sustainable and impact investing, offering various clean energy funds. An established player, based on Netherlands with 30 years experience of ethical investing. 
- [Wealthify](https://invest.wealthify.com/CreatePlan/PensionTransfer): Ethical investment platform with several clean energy focused investment options. The link is for a simplified way of displaying your options. A helpful tool to use either way. 

Here’s a few in Spain (we have less intel on Spain, so please see these as a starting point):
- [Invercaixa](https://www.caixabank.es/bancapremier/fondos-de-inversion/gama-si.html): Offers socially responsible investment (SRI) funds focused on environmental, social, and governance (ESG) factors, including some with a clean energy focus.
- [BBVA](https://www.bbva.com/en/sustainability/bbvas-sustainable-solutions-for-a-green-future/): Provides SRI funds with various themes, including ecology and renewable energy.  

### Week 2: Initiate the transfer (if applicable) 

If your current provider doesn’t offer what you want and you have the option to move your pension out (often this is only for pensions with previous employers), then let your provider know you wish to move your money. 

- Contact your current provider: Inform them of your intention to transfer your pension. This can sometimes mean a phone call, but more and more it can be done online, depending on your provider (e.g. [Pension Bee’s Fossil Fuel Free](https://www.pensionbee.com/fossil-fuel-free-pension) is online) [8] . You’ll need your policy number, your National Insurance (for UK) and then obviously all personal information. 
- Request transfer forms (if applicable): Obtain and complete the necessary forms provided by your current and new providers.
- Choose a transfer type (if applicable): Discuss options like full, partial, or phased transfers (depending on the providers).
- Be aware of fees: Check for potential transfer fees associated with the process.

### Week 3 & 4: Patience

Then it’s really a waiting game until the transfer comes through. This can be anywhere from one to 12 weeks! 

Taking this step might feel daunting, but remember, you're far from being alone with more and more people all over the world taking their money out of fossil fuels. Please don’t hesitate to get in touch with the Something team if you want to discuss. Go get ‘em!
