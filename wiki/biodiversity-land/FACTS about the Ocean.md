## 1.  FACTS
🌊 **==510 million square kilometres: that's the total surface area of the Earth! More than 70% of which is covered by water.==**

By absorbing  **90% of human-created global warming, and 25% to 30% of our CO2 emissions**, the ocean is one of our best allies in the fight against climate change!

So, how does it work? It’s down to two processes: physics and biology.

1. The first works thanks to the solubility of CO2 in water. The CO2 contained in the atmosphere dissolves naturally in the ocean, a process improved in low temperatures.As cold water is denser, it sinks, taking the dissolved CO2 with it.
2. The second process is based on photosynthesis by phytoplankton. These microscopic algae absorb CO2 from the atmosphere and convert it into organic matter and oxygen (O2) using sunlight.

When they die, some of them get dragged to the bottom of the oceans, along with its carbon.

"We often hear that forests have a major impact on carbon. But so do the oceans, thanks to the 'sea forest' that is phytoplankton. And while forests take a very long time to regenerate, algal biomass can be re-formed in a matter of days," explains Cécile Guieu, a researcher at the Villefranche Oceanography Laboratory (LOV).

### About Whales

**Blue whales** live 90 years, travelled hundreds of thousands of miles, and absord the equivalent of 33tons of carbon in their lifetime - https://www.oneearth.org/great-whales-sequester-more-carbon-than-previously-thought/ 

> [!NOTE] Did you Know? 
> Blue whale nearly went totally extint. From 350,000 hundreds of years ago to 4,700 in 2001. 4 out of 5 Blue Whale disappeared. Today, it is estimated around 15,000 but recovering of its population is a slow process. 

Whales hunting goes back a long way. In the 1800s, it was an exxential commodity to get oil which was used for lighting, but in the 1900s this argument did not hold up any more. . It also reached a pick in the 1960s, where it is extimated that 700,00 whales were killed in that decade alone. 

As a result, the 1970's song (whales sounds) and the 1975 greenpeace stunt (video) were 2 defining moments to change the public opinion and pave the way to a ban of whale hunting. It has recovered greatly ever since, and there are positive signs, but today, the biggest risks are shipping routes and plastic garbage islnads (mostly discarded fishing nets) on the same route whale take, responisible for indirect killing. 

As a result, the Dominican republic is the first to implement restriction on shipping routs to her island, acknowledging the co-benfits sperm whales in this case, have on the overall marin ecosystem, resulting in more plankton, which capture carbon and feed life which result in better sustainable fishing for the community.

### Phytoplankton - an essential part of the food chain
Used as food for Blue Whales, responsible of the biggest vetical migration as fishes and organism from the deep come up to feed on, Phytoplankton also play an essential role in growing with the sun and capturing CO2 in the process, then syncing to be absorbed by its feeders or fall all the way down to the deep and sequestrate its CO2 for millenias in the process. 


> [!NOTE] Did You Know?
> Studies show that Phytoplankton not only contribute to half of all Oxygen in our atmosphere but they alos capture an estimated 40% of all additional CO2 that we have produced. 

- Sperm whale feed in the deep but defecate at the surface, poo containing iron, nitrogen which fertilise at the surface in the contact of sunlight, fueling blooms of Phytoplankton!
