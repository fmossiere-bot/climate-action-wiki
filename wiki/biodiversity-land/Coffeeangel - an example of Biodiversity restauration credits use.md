### Summary
Irish speciality coffee group Coffeeangel is joining forces with the Dunsany Estate in Co Meath and cleantech company Bionua in an initiative that will see it commit 5 per cent of profits over the next five years to buying biodiversity credits. This is the first time such a deal has been completed in Ireland – and only the second time across Europe."
##### Coffeeangel invest 5% of its profits in biodiversity credits from rewilded nature reserve

> Restoring biodiversity can bring a double dividend, helping to bring ecosystems back to a healthy state and countering many of the consequences of global warming. It has a critical role in making a sustainable world.


### About Biodiversity Credits
>  Biodiversity credits are legal and financial instruments, serving as tradable assets. Their value is primarily derived from their ability to facilitate absorbing carbon from the atmosphere and significantly bolster biodiversity. They are equivalent to an eco-friendly certificate that shows you have made a piece of land healthier for nature, allowing it to support more plants and animals as well as absorb carbon dioxide. 
### **How is it verified?**

[Bionua](https://bionua.com/) comes in. It deploys cutting-edge technology to monitor nature in real time. Driven by AI, it utilises a combination of satellite multispectral imaging and ground-based sensors to accurately quantify carbon sequestration and biodiversity above and below ground.

# What else is Coffeeangel doing for the environment? Coffeeangel sustainability — full picture

### 1. The Dunsany Nature Reserve partnership (the main thing)

This is still the centrepiece. They've committed 5% of profits over five years to buy Nature Capital Credits from Dunsany Nature Reserve, with the aim to remove and store a minimum of 80,000 kg of carbon in the first year alone. They frame it not just as offsetting, but as actively contributing to restoration. The credits are verified using three ISO standards:

- **ISO 14064-1** — organisational carbon management (how a company measures and reports its own emissions)
- **ISO 14064-2** — carbon and biodiversity quantification at project level (how the specific sequestration at Dunsany is calculated)
- **ISO 14064-3** — verification and validation of carbon data by a third party

Monitoring is backed by satellite multispectral imaging, ground-based sensors, and AI analysis through their partner Bionua.

### 2. Disposable cups — ReCup with EarthCoating® technology

Their disposable cups use EarthCoating® technology, which makes them fully recyclable through Ireland's existing paper recycling system — so they become new products rather than waste. This is notable because they explicitly reject the "compostable cup" route — they looked into compostable cups but found that these require commercial composting to actually break down, and most end up in general landfill anyway — on top of being almost 300% more expensive than conventional single-use cups. So their position is: pick something that actually works in the real world rather than something that sounds good on paper.

### 3. Reusable cups — (YourCupOurFuture)

They have a long-running reusable cup campaign. Rather than just giving a discount for bringing a reusable cup (which they considered and rejected), they created an initiative called *YourCupOurFuture*: every time a customer buys a coffee using a reusable cup, Coffeeangel donates 20¢ to Friends of the Earth Ireland on their behalf. The idea was to create bigger community impact rather than just an individual transaction incentive.


### 4. Industry advocacy and awareness

Through the program, they also worked to raise public awareness and fund research and education on environmental issues, and they've engaged in policy development and industry best practices. They say they were doing this before sustainability became a mainstream talking point in the Irish coffee industry.

### 5. Ireland's biodiversity crisis — the context

The Coffeeangel article sets out why this kind of initiative matters in an Irish context:

- 85% of protected habitats in Ireland are in unfavourable condition.
- Farmland bird populations have declined by 50% since the 1970s.
- Over 30% of Ireland's semi-natural grasslands have been lost in the last decade.
- 1 in 5 assessed species is threatened with extinction in Ireland.
- 1 in 3 bee species is threatened with extinction.

As of 2025, Dunsany Nature Reserve has captured and stored **7,153,104.64 kg of CO₂** since the programme began in 2024. Each Nature Capital Unit represents one tonne of carbon removed plus a proportionate share of biodiversity gain, making verifiable claims possible for corporate partners.

### 6. What's NOT there (worth noting)

There's **no mention of Fairtrade, Rainforest Alliance, or any supply chain certification** on their website. Their sustainability story is focused entirely on the local/Irish environmental side — cups, biodiversity, carbon. If you're looking at their sourcing practices (how they treat farmers, where the beans come from), that's not addressed publicly at all. That's a gap compared to other Irish roasters.


**Overall read:** Coffeeangel has a clear, locally-rooted sustainability identity — Dunsany, the cups, the reusable campaign — and they make a genuine point about choosing things that work in the real world rather than just ticking boxes. But it's all about the environmental/local side. Supply chain ethics and coffee farming conditions are a blind spot in their public communications.
