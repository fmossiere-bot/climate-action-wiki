#wind-energy #ireland 

Ireland Energy reduction goals by 2030 and 2050 will rely mostly on Wind energy. Although [[DRAFT - IRELAND Energy and Biomass]] will also play a role to substitute gas. Our goals are 
- reduce energy industry emission by 60% and have 80% of energy from renewable sources by 2030
- Today Ireland has a capacity of 4.5GW of renewable energy. By 2050, we will need 25 GW
- Our electricity demand will grow by 3-4 times, from 29TWh to 84-122TWh. 
- GhG from Ireladn around 60Mt today, must reduce to close to 0
- Today energy system dominated by Fossil Fuel
- Ireland use of wind, around 10TWh in 2019
- Renewable energy is around 12%, for electricity it is 37%

### Path to 90% of energy supplied by Wind energy
Path to multiply from 10TWh output to more than 77TWh in 2050, representing majority of renewable source. This would require between 41GW to 46GW of deployment
- Currently has a bit less than 1GW capacity of offshore capacity, with plans from EDF to bring to about 3GW by 2030. This is to compare with the 93 GW new capacity brought in in 2022. 
- By 2050, irish wind could contribute to 2.5% of the EU Electricity demand, and over 5% of the Wind energy generation.
