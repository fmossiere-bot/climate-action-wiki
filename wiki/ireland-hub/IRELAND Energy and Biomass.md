### End of Peat-Fired Era
- Traditionally, Irealnd would have heavuily relied on Peat bruning for heating homes and generating power. This is because it had abundance of peatlands, and peat-fired era beginned during WWII as it become a National Policy
- unfortunately, Peatlands have been degraded as a result, something that Ireland is trying to reverse with a plan to restore/ [https://m.independent.ie/irish-news/era-of-peat-fired-electricity-ends-as-edenderry-power-plant-switches-fully-to-burning-biomass/a184309624.html](https://m.independent.ie/irish-news/era-of-peat-fired-electricity-ends-as-edenderry-power-plant-switches-fully-to-burning-biomass/a184309624.html) 
	- This is the reason Ireland has a huge commitment to restore those peatland, as part of our climate action plan 
- The last Peat-Fired plant has closed, with Edenderry power plant switches fully to burning biomass ([The Independent](Edenderry power plant switches fully to burning biomass))

### Biomethane has the potential to replace 1/4 of Natural Gaz in Ireland
Source: https://www.rte.ie/news/business/2023/0907/1403815-biomethane-could-replace-a-quarter-of-natural-gas-use/

Biomethane, which is carbon neutral and renewable, could also reduce Ireland's total emissions by almost 4 million tonnes a year, representing 6.5% of Ireland’s total emissions.

###### Currently there are 176 projects throughout Ireland which if delivered could produce 14.8 terawatt hours of biomethane a year.

> While being controversial, it just shows the interest in this abudnant Irish alternative. But making sure that the sources are sustainable is of utmost importance

- "While the first Irish policy support for biomethane is in development, it is essential that it provides a framework that delivers long-term price certainty for biomethane producers and ensures that the planning and permitting process is aligned to the specific needs of the sector," Mr Marley said.
- However, Friends of the Earth Ireland said it is skeptical about the Gas Networks Ireland predictions and and pointed out that while the Government is currently looking to increase biomethane in Ireland, this cannot come at the expense of sustainability or Ireland's climate commitments to rapidly reduce fossil fuels.
- Stable pricing is also really important

### Gaz Network ireland is working on a plan to buy Irish Biomethane
Source: https://www.irishtimes.com/business/2024/06/07/gas-networks-ireland-weighs-up-domestic-biomethane-plan/

> ## State company’s move to purchase Irish-produced renewable gas follows publication of Government strategy

" “Working in consultation with the Commission for the Regulation of Utilities [CRU], Gas Networks Ireland will be going to market and a formal process to procure biomethane will commence shortly,"

###### Government strategy aims to replace 10 per cent of the natural gas that the Republic uses with biomethane, made by processing farming and food waste.


> [!NOTE] Ireland has huge amount of farming waste
> Irish biomethane production trails EU countries such as Denmark, despite having large quantities of the farming waste used to make the gas.

> Government hopes its strategy will aid the construction of up to 200 biomethane production plants, called anaerobic digesters, in rural communities.
