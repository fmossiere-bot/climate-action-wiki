# PLAN
Source: https://www.npws.ie/legislation/national-biodiversity-action-plan

**The two main official sources are:**
1. **gov.ie** — the government's own publication page: `gov.ie/en/department-of-housing-local-government-and-heritage/publications/irelands-4th-national-biodiversity-action-plan-20232030/`
2. **npws.ie** (National Parks & Wildlife Service) — this is where you can download the full plan document directly, both in English and Irish. NPWS is essentially the body that runs the plan, so it's a legitimate and authoritative source.


> npws.ie is  the official home of the plan, hosted by the government body responsible for delivering it.


Here's a simple overview of Ireland's biodiversity plan: **Ireland's 4th National Biodiversity Action Plan (2023–2030)**

It was published in January 2024 by the Department of Housing, Local Government and Heritage.

## **Why does it exist?**

> The situation is quite bad. Almost a third of EU-protected species and 85% of EU-protected habitats in Ireland are in poor condition. More than half of native Irish plant species have declined in the last 20 years, and a fifth of breeding bird species are also declining.

## **What does the plan include?**

It contains 194 actions to tackle biodiversity loss, to be completed by 2030.

The five main goals are:

- A "whole of government, whole of society" approach
- Meeting urgent conservation and restoration needs
- Protecting nature's contribution to people (clean water, food, etc.)
- Building a better evidence base (data and monitoring)
- Strengthening Ireland's contribution to international biodiversity efforts

## **What makes it different from previous plans?**

This is the first plan to be published on a statutory basis — meaning the Wildlife Amendment Act 2023 now legally requires public bodies to incorporate biodiversity into their plans and policies.

**In short:** it's Ireland's main roadmap to stop and reverse the loss of nature by 2030, and for the first time it has legal teeth behind it.

## Some of the Key Actions
Here are 5 key actions from the plan:

**1. Restore and rewet peatlands (bogs)** Restoring and rehabilitating Ireland's peatlands is one of the headline actions [Biodiversityconference](https://www.biodiversityconference.ie/news/actions-for-nature-irelands-national-biodiversity-action-plan-is-published/), including rewetting raised bogs that have been damaged over decades.

**2. Create Marine Protected Areas** The government will develop legislation on Marine Protected Areas (MPAs) and rapidly progress its implementation, with a target of 30% protection of the marine environment by 2030. [Irishriverproject](https://irishriverproject.com/2022/09/05/public-consultation-on-irelands-4th-national-biodiversity-action-plan/)

**3. Tackle invasive species more aggressively** A dedicated invasive alien species unit will be established to speed up enforcement, and on-the-ground work will remove invasive species like Japanese knotweed and rhododendron from native woodlands and peatlands. [Irishriverproject](https://irishriverproject.com/2022/09/05/public-consultation-on-irelands-4th-national-biodiversity-action-plan/)

**4. Make farming more nature-friendly** More nature-friendly farming measures will be introduced through Ireland's agricultural schemes, with a target to reduce pesticide use by 50% by 2030. [Irishriverproject](https://irishriverproject.com/2022/09/05/public-consultation-on-irelands-4th-national-biodiversity-action-plan/)

**5. Expand and protect National Parks** Actions include expanding National Parks, strengthening efforts on wildlife crime, and working with local communities to protect habitats. [Naturebasedsolutionsinitiative](https://www.naturebasedsolutionsinitiative.org/news/ireland-nbap/)

These are the broad headline actions — the full plan has 194 in total, covering everything from urban green spaces to ocean protection.

## How Ireland's industrial estates can help save our bees 
Source: https://www.rte.ie/brainstorm/2026/0318/1561658-bees-rewilding-industrial-estates-nature-pollinating/

- Over 30 of Ireland's 100 native bee species are now threatened with extinction [rte](https://www.rte.ie/brainstorm/2026/0318/1561658-bees-rewilding-industrial-estates-nature-pollinating/), making the need for action urgent.
- Industrial estates, which cover a large portion of Ireland's developed land, have been completely ignored in national biodiversity conversations [rte](https://www.rte.ie/brainstorm/2026/0318/1561658-bees-rewilding-industrial-estates-nature-pollinating/) — yet they could form a network of pollinator corridors across the country.
- Researchers at Maynooth University created a practical guide for industrial estates, with three levels of action: quick wins like bee hotels and no-mow patches, a mid-level building-up phase, and more ambitious rewilding efforts [rte](https://www.rte.ie/brainstorm/2026/0318/1561658-bees-rewilding-industrial-estates-nature-pollinating/) — designed to work in real-world conditions, not just ideal ones.


## Ponds put under microscope as new study probes their role in supporting pollinators
Source: [The Independant]([https://m.independent.ie/regionals/wexford/business/farming/ponds-put-under-microscope-as-new-study-probes-their-role-in-supporting-pollinators/a65161652.html#](https://m.independent.ie/regionals/wexford/business/farming/ponds-put-under-microscope-as-new-study-probes-their-role-in-supporting-pollinators/a65161652.html#))


![A pond at McCalls Farm in Kildare.](https://focus.independent.ie/thumbor/1cZ2TNO_DiNt4NeufDgTJK19wM4=/0x0:3238x2158/960x640/prod-mh-ireland/678c84be-8d03-4443-8aea-bc005b1fdba8/d917564f-97d9-44d7-a3e1-26ca58d229cb/678c84be-8d03-4443-8aea-bc005b1fdba8.jpg)

> A new four‑year research project is set to shine a light on an overlooked feature of Irish farmland, the humble pond, and its potential to support struggling pollinator species.

The plight of pollinators has become so severe that County Wicklow is now home to Ireland’s first dedicated Bee Sanctuary, created in response to the alarming decline in populations.

The Ponds for Pollinators Project will assess how features such as pond size, age and surrounding vegetation influence pollinator numbers and diversity on working farms. The study will also explore why farmers choose to create ponds and what barriers may prevent others from doing the same.
