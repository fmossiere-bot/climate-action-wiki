## Powerscourt need to replace 80% of its hedges
*==Source: [Irishtimes]([https://www.irishtimes.com/ireland/2024/07/09/box-tree-caterpillar-reshapes-irish-gardens-as-climate-shift-brings-new-problems/?utm_source=newsshowcase&utm_medium=gnews&utm_campaign=CDAqEAgAKgcICjCp-oULMPu_gwMwmdiyAg&utm_content=bullets](https://www.irishtimes.com/ireland/2024/07/09/box-tree-caterpillar-reshapes-irish-gardens-as-climate-shift-brings-new-problems/?utm_source=newsshowcase&utm_medium=gnews&utm_campaign=CDAqEAgAKgcICjCp-oULMPu_gwMwmdiyAg&utm_content=bullets))==*

> Box tree caterpillar reshapes Irish gardens as climate shift brings new problems
> Powerscourt Estate replaces up to 80% of familiar hedge plants in face of moths and blight

Box tree moth or its destructive caterpillar form on their site and their destructive impact is inevitable

> The box tree moth originates from east Asia but was first found on mainland Europe in 2007 and was first found in Ireland in 2017. It is presumed that it was transported on infected boxwood plants


> [!NOTE] It lacks Predators
> The caterpillars feed on both the leaves and the bark of the box tree, which kills the plant. It lacks predators to control it naturally in this country.

Powerscourt Estate have replaced 500m of box tree plants with dwarf Japanese yew hedging. The gardeners recommend not to plant box trees any more.

> The Wicklow estate has taken this approach despite not having the box tree moth present in the gardens as it moves to be more sustainable and the chemical used to kill the moths is highly toxic. “It is rife around Dublin. If we did get it we wouldn’t be spraying chemicals,” said Mr Slazenger.

Climate change has impacted on the fungal diseases and pests they find on the estate. “We were once reliant on very cold winters to kill the blight.”

Powerscourt Estate has taken a number of “preventative measure” to avoid the arrival of box tree moths. Mr Slazenger stressed how the moths can “decimate plants overnight”.
