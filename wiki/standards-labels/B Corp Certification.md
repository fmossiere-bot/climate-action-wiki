[B Corp](https://www.bcorporation.net/en-us/certification/) has become one of the most recognised marks of responsible business. But what does it actually mean to be certified, and who is it really for?


**1. What is B Corp?**

B Corp certification is awarded to companies that meet **verified standards of social and environmental performance, accountability, and transparency.** It is managed by B Lab, a global non-profit organisation founded in the United States in 2006.

> Unlike traditional ESG reporting, which often relies on self-reported metrics, B Corp demands verified, measurable outcomes across five key areas: **governance, workers, community, environment, and customers**. This makes it more demanding than most voluntary sustainability frameworks, and is a large part of why it has built the reputation it has.

**It is worth being clear about what B Corp is not. It does not certify products or services as sustainable**. It assesses the overall management and practices of a business as a whole, and it requires companies to embed stakeholder accountability into their legal structure, not just their communications.


**2. Why is B Corp considered a reference standard?**

Several things set B Corp apart from other sustainability labels.

- **First, it is holistic**. Rather than focusing on a single issue such as carbon emissions or fair trade, **it looks at the entire business**, including how it treats employees, how it engages with its community, how transparent it is with customers, and how its governance is structured.
- **Second, it requires independent verification**. Companies cannot self-certify. B Lab reviews the answers and supporting documents before awarding a score.
- **Third, it requires a legal commitment.** To obtain and maintain B Corp certification, companies must integrate B Corp commitments to all stakeholders into their corporate governing documents, and they must re-certify every three years. This makes the commitment structural rather than cosmetic.

Finally, the standards are evolving. In June 2026, a first group of companies achieved B Corp certification under B Lab's newly revised global standards, described as the most significant overhaul of the certification in its history. The new framework requires companies to meet independently verified performance thresholds across seven impact topics, from climate action to human rights. This tightening of standards is a direct response to concerns that the original system was too flexible, and reflects B Lab's ambition to keep the certification credible as more large corporations pursue it.


**3. Who has adopted it?**

> The B Corp community now spans over 10,800 certified companies across 102 countries and 163 industries, collectively employing more than one million people.

Adoption ranges from small independent businesses to well-known global brands. Large international companies to have gained certification include Nespresso, San Pellegrino, and Innocent Drinks. Patagonia, the outdoor clothing company, has been B Corp certified since 2012 and is one of the most cited examples of what the certification stands for in practice.

The **fastest growth in recent years has been in Europe**, where the combination of growing consumer awareness and tightening regulation, particularly through the EU's CSRD and the Empowering Consumers for the Green Transition Directive, has pushed sustainability up the agenda for many businesses.


> **About B Corp Certification**
> B Corp Certification is a designation that a business is meeting high standards of verified performance, accountability, and transparency on factors from employee benefits and charitable giving to supply chain practices and input materials. In order to achieve certification, a company must: 
> - Demonstrate **high** **social and environmental** **performance** by achieving a B Impact Assessment score of 80 or above and passing our risk review. Multinational corporations must also meet baseline requirement standards. 
> - Make a **legal commitment** by changing their corporate governance structure to be accountable to all stakeholders, not just shareholders, and achieve benefit corporation status if available in their jurisdiction. 
> - Exhibit **transparency** by allowing information about their performance measured against B Lab’s standards to be publicly available on their B Corp profile on B Lab’s website.



**4. The certification process**

The process is structured around the B Impact Assessment, an online questionnaire that covers five areas: governance, workers, community, environment, and customers. Companies answer questions and submit supporting evidence across all five.

> The minimum score required to qualify is 80 out of 200. Companies that reach 80 points undergo an assessment review process, essentially a verification call and document review, before being certified. The median score for companies that complete the assessment without seeking certification is around 50, which gives a sense of how high the bar is.

The pathway to certification varies depending on company size. Smaller businesses follow a simplified process, while larger enterprises with more complex structures face additional requirements and sector-specific standards.

Certification is valid for three years, after which companies must re-apply and demonstrate continued improvement. Annual fees are based on company revenue.


**5. B Corp in Ireland**

Ireland has seen significant growth in B Corp adoption over a short period. In 2018, Ireland celebrated its first certified B Corp. By 2025, more than 90 Irish companies had joined the global B Corp community.

- B Lab Ireland was founded in 2023 to oversee the growth of the movement in Ireland. Over 500 companies have used B Lab's impact measurement tools in the past 12 months, and many more are currently working through the certification process.
- There is also state support available. Enterprise Ireland, LEO, and IDA clients can apply for the GreenStart grant to hire an experienced B Corp consultant, with the funding covering 80% of the cost up to 5,000 euros.
- Irish B Corp companies currently range from coffee roasters and food producers to distilleries and professional services firms. The community is still relatively small, but the pace of growth suggests it is moving from niche to mainstream.



**Bottom line**

B Corp is one of the few sustainability certifications that assesses the whole business, requires legal commitment, and demands independent verification. Its credibility comes precisely from the fact that it is not easy to obtain. For companies serious about demonstrating responsible practices, it remains one of the most recognised frameworks available, and in Ireland, it is growing quickly.
