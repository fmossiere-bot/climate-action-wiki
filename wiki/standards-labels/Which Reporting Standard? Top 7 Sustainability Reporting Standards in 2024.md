### [UPDATE] Towards a more Harmonised landscape? May be
Source: [What is happening in Sustainability and ESG](https://mail.google.com/mail/u/0/#inbox/FMfcgzQVxHkmPXCLsFHnKKJfjVNGQMxC)

|**The International Sustainability Standards Board (ISSB) announced that it is working on** [**harmonizing**](https://link.mail.beehiiv.com/ss/c/u001.eDkfFYt2CB6QlwacYiU4FrIV76IsUtZSfMarEFqI-VF6ih0GAtbJkfNqtlYmc357954AQ6AYJKWJpWIGdFSP2y2UCHZvkQJpF5vznARizZXZC5TdMvv9TLLFZwUzlHJDcRaGvCWUCUUQRgnnxpOHkN9Bv0xo2FlMYBGXcQaF7NVgnS7gfWcma0bokVLihbqvGphLKwZXZowjfF6KOuNPeDGv9xQGgqnTbi-skXsaW7b0EoJ6n6ri8LgE-2DJRa9_KZAzXfqzFsLmQqabVmHJZ0jdIXisjFsty0Pqly4V8QkId29x5P4EqYKlbrlUFM5tkZlR8yp_0GAjq_UkyhfDZWHsjlNQkgSEZtEZc3JQtom9Owo1-ytAz4Ki_-WbFun1/47i/MwMxoL3eS4iYBLp6xz3gvQ/h6/h001.FwBuE35KP_loQW9HsGlOOmN5PVvT-mt6w-XiTYJxejY) **the sustainability reporting landscape****.** The organization is embarking on a new two-year work plan, focusing on reducing the complexity of sustainability reporting initiatives and ensuring high-quality, comparable information about sustainability-related risks and opportunities. The ISSB is partnering with the Transition Plan Taskforce (TPT), GHG Protocol, CDP, Taskforce on Nature-related Financial Disclosures (TNFD), and Global Reporting Initiative (GRI) to achieve this. Over 20 jurisdictions, accounting for nearly 55% of global GDP and more than 40% of global market capitalization, are already using or planning to introduce ISSB Standards in their legal or regulatory frameworks.|

![📑](https://fonts.gstatic.com/s/e/notoemoji/15.0/1f4d1/32.png) **The TNFD and EFRAG have also jointly published a** [**mapping**](https://link.mail.beehiiv.com/ss/c/u001.bnP_cLsPzS1-f2Gfw19rZJ9GvpAJEOflJyAb1psg4KYmeVaw4ZU9pbzhgZ6MOtKvQhptN2edJubBMPwsax7MM0aotsaUw4Btbx_zcpiXt_4nAYV_hdEDTG2P5CSal2WA_yT4As6J_3h20mwCh7fNwpMM9bpyFyorHBYWfItgDfrDCoMozD9sQjTZEVvINyuxbFjJRJYdldZnnNAdiKeDcgAxm4V5o_SagVsKfpOlsQtk2c7whNh5nOkg6C9SwepsYP5rJl1zeusqLHbjK0yR3A/47i/MwMxoL3eS4iYBLp6xz3gvQ/h7/h001.rJYN7cQM8tvm2CRASiGIaVM2c1f3liZ7FUaYonBVLPQ) **of the correspondence between the European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS) and the TNFD’s recommended disclosures and metrics.** This mapping aims to help companies understand the commonalities between ESRS and TNFD, aiding those in the scope of the CSRD that consider alignment with TNFD. The high level of correspondence is reflected through concepts and definitions, the approach to materiality, the LEAP approach, reporting pillars, and recommended disclosures and metrics.|
