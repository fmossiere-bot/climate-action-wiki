## Update 2023
In 2023, Gold Standard delivered unprecedented impact and created a record 10.3 billion dollars of shared value through our projects and programmes, increasing from 5.8 billion dollars in 2022. We added 404 new projects to our pipeline, up from 114 in 2022, and we reached the milestone of 1,500 fully certified projects since our inception. Despite the concerns in the market, we also retired 29 million credits, an increase from 2022, demonstrating continued demand for high quality amidst increased scrutiny.  
  
2023 was a turbulent year for the voluntary carbon market and Article 6 progress, but also one with many promising advancements and innovation made through rigorous efforts and collaborations by Governments, private sector, academic communities and NGOs. Gold Standard will continue to uphold the highest level of credibility and rigour as the market evolves, and deliver ever greater amounts of sustainable impact in a credible way.  
  
Our innovative work in the sustainable finance space made substantial progress with the first funds achieving design certification under our portfolio requirements . Our adaptation framework piloting is also picking up, with ongoing engagements to secure the first pilot of performance certification. This reflects the growing importance of climate adaptation and just transition to the world. Finally, our work to support the corporate transition to global net zero was advanced through our 'Fairly Contributing to Global Net Zero' framework. Our global challenge will require efforts from every corner of the globe and Gold Standard is excited to be part of this journey with our partners.  
  
As CEO of Gold Standard, I would like to thank our community for their trust, support and advice over the last year and wish all of us another dynamic, collaborative, and courageous year to contribute towards climate security and sustainable development for all.
