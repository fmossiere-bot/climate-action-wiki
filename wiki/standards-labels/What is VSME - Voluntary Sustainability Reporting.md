## What About Smaller Businesses (Micro-Undertakings)?

Micro-undertakings are highly welcome to use this standard. Rather than completing the full framework, the standard outlines a tailored approach for them:

- **Target Approach:** Micro-undertakings are encouraged to fill out **only the Basic Module**.
- **Omission Flexibility:** They can easily choose to apply only the specific parts that are explicitly relevant to their immediate operational activities.


    

## Why Should an SME Voluntarily Adopt It?

While the standard has **no direct legal authority** and these businesses fall completely outside the mandatory scope of the EU's Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD), there are critical commercial reasons to adopt it:

- **Supply Chain Pressures:** Large corporate clients facing mandatory EU reporting are heavily requesting sustainability data from their suppliers. VSME helps SMEs satisfy those vendor requests.
- **Access to Capital:** Banks and investors increasingly require ESG risk profiles to grant loans or equity investments. VSME standardizes this data to assist in securing corporate finance.
- **Competitive Growth:** Standardizing management around challenges like energy, pollution, and workforce safety enhances mid-to-long-term business resilience.

## What Does the Framework Entail?

The framework is structured into modular building blocks that must be complied with in their entirety once chosen:

1. The Basic Module (Minimum Requirement)
- **General Information (B1â€“B2):** Legal structure, NACE industry sector codes, basic corporate metrics, and a description of existing sustainability practices, transition policies, or targets.
- **Environmental Metrics (B3â€“B7):** Energy consumption (MWh), gross Scope 1 and location-based Scope 2 greenhouse gas emissions ($tCO_2eq$), pollution tracking (if legally monitored), land-use/biodiversity sites, water withdrawal, and circular economy/waste generation breakdown.
- **Social Metrics (B8â€“B10):** Workforce characteristics (gender, contract type), rate of work-related accidents/fatalities, minimum wage alignment, training hours, and unadjusted gender pay gaps.
- **Governance Metrics (B11):** Tracking of formal court convictions and regulatory fines related to corruption and bribery.


2. The Comprehensive Module

This is an advanced layer built directly on top of the Basic Module, specifically targeting standard disclosures that financial markets, banks, and major clients request. It expands into:

- **Value Chain Carbon:** Quantification of Scope 3 emissions (indirect value chain impacts) and absolute short/long-term reduction targets.
- **Climate Risks (C4):** Defining physical hazards (e.g., floods, acute weather) and transition events affecting financial positioning.
- **Human Rights (C6â€“C7):** Documenting explicit codes of conduct regarding child labor, forced labor, discrimination, and tracking formal complaints or human rights incidents.
- **Sector Exclusions & Diversity (C8â€“C9):** Disclosing revenues sourced from specific sectors like fossil fuels, controversial weapons, or tobacco, alongside board gender-diversity ratios.


## Timelines

- **Standard Publication:** The finalized text for this voluntary standard was published in **December 2024**.
- **The Second Year of Reporting:** Comparative information tracking data from the previous fiscal year becomes a required fixture starting from an undertaking's second consecutive year of utilizing the standard.
- **Gender Pay Gap Threshold adjustment:** For social metric reporting (B10), businesses under 150 employees may completely omit the gender pay gap breakdown. However, this exemption threshold shrinks down to entities with fewer than **100 employees starting on June 7, 2031**.

> The December 2024 timeline marks a specific milestone in the administrative process:


- **Final Draft Submission:** On December 17, 2024, EFRAG finalized the technical standard document and officially submitted it to the European Commission. While it was published at that time and ready for immediate voluntary use/adoption by businesses, it lacked official regulatory status.

 Subsequent Milestones:

- **Official Recommendation (July 2025):** The European Commission officially endorsed and adopted EFRAG's framework as an **official Commission Recommendation** on July 30, 2025. This endorsement encouraged banks, investors, and large corporations to mirror their ESG supply chain data requests against the VSME framework.
- **Delegated Act Process (2026):** Because the VSME standard is entirely **voluntary**, it does not "come into effect" with mandatory compliance deadlines like a directive. However, the European Commission is currently conducting a public consultation (running through June 2026) to formally adopt a revised version of this voluntary standard as an official delegated act under EU law.

Essentially, December 2024 was when the standard was technically finished and handed over to the EU; its life as an officially recognized framework began in mid-2025.
