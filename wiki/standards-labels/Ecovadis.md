As companies face growing pressure to demonstrate responsible sourcing, [EcoVadis](https://ecovadis.com/ ) has become one of the most widely used tools to assess and communicate sustainability performance across global supply chains.

---

**1. What is EcoVadis?**

EcoVadis is a SaaS platform that provides sustainability ratings for companies. Its core purpose is to help organisations assess ESG risks across their supply chains, which on average account for around 90% of a company's total environmental impact.

The rating covers four main areas: environment, labour and human rights, ethics, and sustainable procurement. Companies are scored on the strength of their sustainability management systems, not just on outputs or outcomes.

EcoVadis is not a certification body in the traditional sense. It does not certify products or services as sustainable. What it does is assess how well a company manages sustainability across its operations and supply chain, relative to others in the EcoVadis database.

---

**2. Who is it for?**

EcoVadis is designed for two types of users.

The first is companies that are asked by a client or partner to complete an assessment, typically as part of a supplier qualification or procurement process. Being rated on EcoVadis has become a standard requirement in many industries, particularly in manufacturing, retail, and technology.

The second is companies that want to assess and manage the sustainability performance of their own suppliers. In this context, EcoVadis functions as a supply chain risk management tool, giving procurement teams a structured way to monitor ESG risks across their vendor base.

Providers such as Sustainabl-IT offer both access to the EcoVadis certification process and the broader SaaS platform, supporting companies through the assessment and beyond.

---

**3. What is the process?**

The process follows a few clear steps.

A company is invited to complete an EcoVadis questionnaire, either by a client requesting proof of sustainability performance or by choosing to be assessed independently. The questionnaire covers policies, actions, and results across the four areas mentioned above, and is adapted based on the company's sector and size.

Supporting documents are submitted alongside the answers. These are reviewed by EcoVadis analysts, who produce a scorecard with a rating out of 100.

Based on the score, companies may be awarded a medal. There are four levels: Bronze, Silver, Gold, and Platinum, awarded to companies that score within the top 50%, 25%, 5%, and 1% of all assessed companies respectively.


> **An EcoVadis medal or badge is an acknowledgement of achievement _relative_ to other assessed companies across the EcoVadis database.**

It is worth noting that a medal is an indicator of relative performance, not an absolute guarantee of sustainability. EcoVadis itself is clear that a medal reflects positive intent and the quality of a management system, and that there is always room for improvement.

Assessments are renewed annually, and subscription pricing varies depending on the size of the company and the features required, from basic ratings to premium plans that include badge display and more detailed analytics.


> **What are Ecovadis medals and Badges**
> EcoVadis Medals and Badges recognize eligible companies that have completed the EcoVadis assessment process and demonstrated a relatively strong management system that addresses sustainability criteria, as outlined in the **[EcoVadis methodology](https://support.ecovadis.com/hc/en-us/articles/115002531507)**.
> An EcoVadis medal or badge is **NOT** a certification or an endorsement of a company or its products or services, and it does not indicate that the company’s products or services are specifically sustainable or more sustainable than another company’s products or services.
> An earned medal or badge does not mean that a company has no room for improvement or any potential weaknesses in their sustainability business practices. It serves as an indicator of positive intent as assessed companies strive to improve their sustainability management performance and drive impact.


---

**Bottom line**

EcoVadis has become a practical standard for sustainability due diligence in global supply chains. For many companies, having a rating is no longer optional. It is increasingly a condition of doing business with larger buyers, particularly in Europe.
