> - Carbon is different from CO2 emissions. Carbon stock means accumulation of Carbon from the emissions of CO2. 40GT of CO2 emissions increaed the Carbon stock by around 4.9GT once we take into acccount the 55% removed thansk to our sink
> - Total Carbon stock today is 880Gt, for a concentration of 412ppm

#### We have a budget of 420GT over 10 years before we reach 1.5degree (66% chance)

Think about a bathtub. Carbon stays in the atmosphere for many years (100s) .. so Net-Zero mean we do not emit any more CO2, and let our natural sink remove slowly Carbon from the atmosphere to mitigate the effect. 


![[Screenshot 2023-09-12 at 11.25.15.png]]

Not all of this extra carbon stays in the air. About a quarter of it ends up in the ocean, and roughly 30 percent is absorbed by plants and trees. This leaves some [44 percent](https://essd.copernicus.org/articles/14/4811/2022/#:~:text=trend%2C%20remaining%20at-,around%2044%E2%80%89%25,-%2C%20albeit%20showing%20a) of our CO2 emissions piling up in the atmosphere, netting out to more than 5 billion metric tons of carbon added every year on average over the last decade. The rate of absorption by vegetation and in the oceans has increased over time, as we have continued adding more carbon to the atmosphere.
