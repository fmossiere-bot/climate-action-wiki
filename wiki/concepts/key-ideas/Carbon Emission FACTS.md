#CarbonFootprint #FACTS 

==**AGRICULTURE**==

-   **Agriculture remains the 3rd largest contributor** to Carbon emissions, after Energy production (Coal) and [Transport.It](http://Transport.It) accounts for at least 25% of all greenhouse gaz emissions
-   Livestock account for a substantial amount of those emissions. Emissions from the 3 main regions (Asia, South America and Africa which account for 80% of total emissions from this source) increased 44% between 1990 and 2010. 
-   Adding all emissions related to livestock together, it represent more than 80% of agriculture emissions. 
-   Livestock are responsible for **14.5% of the total greenhouse** gas emissions, with the majority stemming from milk and beef production. 
     

**_"An obvious way to reduce Agriculture emissions is for Humans to reduce their consumption of meat. "_** 

-   For this reason, reducing meat consumption is a priority. **[This article](https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2019/jun/12/most-meat-in-2040-will-not-come-from-slaughtered-animals-report?CMP=Share_iOSApp_Other)** on the Guardian shows a clear direction for the meat industry.
-   38% of Brazilian beef (which is responsible for Amazon fires to free space) is imported by China. Is China more to blame than Brazil?

==**TRANSPORT**==

-   **Within the European Union, transport contributes to 27% of overall Carbon Dioxide emissions**
-   **Almost 1/2 of that is from private car use**
-   **It is the only sector that has seen CO2 emissions rise over the past 3 decades, by over 1/4**

**Flying**
-   A recent comprehensive analysis has found that the effect of flying on global warming has doubled between 2000 and 2018
-   Overall, flying is responsible for 3.5% of overall global warming effect resulting from human activities.
