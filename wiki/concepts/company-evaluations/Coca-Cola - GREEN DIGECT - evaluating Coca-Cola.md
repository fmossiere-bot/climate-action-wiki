## Where the gaps / tensions lie (possible “greenhushing” or glossing issues)

Putting the two sides together, here are the tension points. These are relevant if you want to assess whether there is under‐communication, over-claiming, or selective messaging.

- **Reuse / refill versus recycling**: Coca-Cola seems to have scaled back a prior commitment toward reuse/refillable packaging in its public goals. The shift is toward recycling and “collection” rather than upstream solutions (i.e. not just what happens after the bottle is used, but how to avoid single-use or design for reuse). This could be a case where they avoid or downplay harder, more disruptive sustainability options.
- **Geographic variation**: What Coca-Cola can promise in Europe (or in markets with strong waste infrastructure, strong regulation) may not map to what is feasible in many other markets. Thus, some claims may be more reflective of “ideal practices” rather than what is happening globally everywhere.
- **Communication clarity**: Labels like “100% recycled / recyclable” are powerful marketing tools, but if consumers misunderstand what exactly is included (caps, labels, or certain components), or whether local recycling infrastructure supports it, then the message may overstate the practical effect.
- **Ambition vs. delivery over time**: Their goals (2035, etc.) are ambitious. The question is whether the pace of progress is sufficient, whether there are binding external verification or accountability, and whether there are interim milestones. If those are weak or not emphasized, stakeholders may view the goals with scepticism.
