## Washing Cold Saves Money Short and Long Term

Switching to cold saves a household anywhere from £50 - £160 ($60 - $200) in energy costs alone each year[3](https://www.basf.com/us/en/media/featured-articles/Sustainability/cold-water-washing.html#accordion_v2-e8a9b4f70b-item-bdb167ad82). Let’s be real, probably more in the UK right now given the price of energy! 

> Beyond energy savings, cold washing also saves money by making your outfits last longer. In just one hot-water wash, fabrics such as cotton will reach their maximum shrinkage capacity.  Jeans, for example, shrink 3% - 4%, which means losing approximately “1″–1¼” in the length[3](https://www.basf.com/us/en/media/featured-articles/Sustainability/cold-water-washing.html#accordion_v2-e8a9b4f70b-item-bdb167ad82). 

Meanwhile, cold washing has no shrinkage effects at all. So save  your favorite outfits and switch to cold!![[Screenshot 2024-10-29 at 12.49.42.png]]

## The hidden environmental costs of washing on hot

##### Carbon emissions associated with washing, drying and ironing account for about a third of the lifetime emissions of clothing (that means including manufacture!)[2](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0143720819320431). 

  That one twist of the knob to cold means reducing CO2 emissions by 850-1600 pounds per household each year[3](https://www.basf.com/us/en/media/featured-articles/Sustainability/cold-water-washing.html#accordion_v2-e8a9b4f70b-item-bdb167ad82)![[Screenshot 2024-10-29 at 12.49.47.png]]
  
Microfibers are also a major source of pollution, and microfibre release is much higher in 40 °C than cold washes[2](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0143720819320431). So all round this one is a no-brainer!

> [!Top Tips & Tricks]
> - Carefully check garment wash instructions before using harsh detergents, temperatures, or settings. Most clothes prefer a gentle cold wash.
> - Look for eco-friendly detergents and use only as much as you need. Some good sheet options [here](https://www.independent.co.uk/extras/indybest/house-garden/eco-friendly-laundry-sheets-detergent-b2088706.html). 
> - Shop for clothes made of natural fibers that don’t require dry cleaning (extra points for pre-loved :)
> - Switch to a clothesline or rack drying if you use a dryer. This can save you significant money and prevent clothes shrinkage. 
> - Sort out the whites and save the hot water for only the most needed washes. Bleach whites only when necessary (once a year can be enough!)
