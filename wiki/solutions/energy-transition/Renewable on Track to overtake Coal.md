## Top 10 countries phasing out coal the fastest
Source: https://www.wri.org/insights/countries-phasing-out-coal-power-fastest


> [!NOTE] COAL is still an important part of the energy mix
> Coal, the most polluting fossil fuel, [supplied 36%](https://ember-climate.org/data/data-tools/data-explorer/) of electricity generation in 2022. This must drop to [4% by 2030](https://climateactiontracker.org/documents/1159/CAT_2023-09-19_Briefing_ParisBenchmarks_PowerSector.pdf) and then 0% by 2040 if the world is to limit global warming to 1.5 degrees C (2.7 degrees F) and prevent the most catastrophic impacts of the climate crisis. (That's according to a high-ambition scenario from [Climate Action Tracker](https://climateactiontracker.org/about/).)

##### Greece reduced coal power faster than any other country in the world over an eight-year span, from 51% in 2014 to 10% in 2022, replacing it with a combination of gas and renewables

![[Screenshot 2025-05-12 at 10.05.35.png]]


A lot of different come into play to understand the shift for those 10 countries, from population, GDP, growth rate, energy dependancies. 

> The nature of each country’s coal industry matters, too. Six of the 10 countries relied mainly on [coal imports](https://yearbook.enerdata.net/coal-lignite/coal-production-data.html), so shifting to other power sources like renewables or domestically available gas could benefit their energy security without causing major economic impacts. However, the U.S., Germany, Romania and Greece were able to reduce coal power dramatically despite having large coal mining sectors and relying on mostly domestic coal production.
