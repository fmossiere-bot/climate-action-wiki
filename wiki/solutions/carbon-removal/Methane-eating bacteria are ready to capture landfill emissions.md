We know that waste plays an important role in Methane emissions, a gaz 80 more potent than CO2. When waste is thrown into landfills, it de-compose itself, a process during which CH4 is emitted. 

###### Waste in landfill is 18% of anthropogenics CH4 emissions

> To solve that problem there are many solutions that exist, from Capturing direcctly the CH4 on the landfill, to using food waste to generate biomass energy instead, (methane digester), and finally to reduce Food waste both at production and at home levels. But this is a new one 


###### Bioreactors housing methane-eating bacteria could offer a portable, off-grid solution for soaking up methane leaks from sites like landfills and coal mines 

Scientist will release bioreactors filled with a specially strained of methane-eating bacterias. it could work, and as they scale up, those bioreactors could capture 24Mt of Co2-e by mid-century.
