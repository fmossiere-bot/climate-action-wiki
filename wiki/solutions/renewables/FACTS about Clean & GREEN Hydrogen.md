> **Green hydrogen** is hydrogen derived from water. It’s created using a process called electrolysis, wherein electricity from renewable sources is used to split the hydrogen molecules from the oxygen molecules in water

> When burnt, Hydrogen mainly release water vapor. 

> **Blue Hydrogen**, while created from Natural gaz, promess to capture CO2  during the production process, at least it is claimed so

## Green hydrogen in the context of Storage of energy
See this Article: https://medium.com/@kitfitton/is-hydrogen-going-to-keep-the-lights-on-in-the-electricity-grid-of-the-future-b67c15e0d389
- [x] Read

Basically, while attractive, there is inefficiency in using Hydrogen as a Storage solution. Between the Electrosis, transport and re-electrification, you will get only 42% return (for every 100 unit of electricity to you put in, only 42 will get back). 

##### "This gives us a total round-trip efficiency (electricity to hydrogen to electricity) of 42%. For every 100 units of electricity that we use to power an electrolyser, we’ll only get 42 units back as electricity when we need it."


Hydrogen can be a very important solution to the seasonal storage problem for systems with high levels of renewable energy, providing critical long-duration storage. 
Very briefly, this is how hydrogen works: You can use solar or wind energy for electrolysis, a process by which you split water into hydrogen and oxygen. You then liquefy and store the hydrogen. At times of low solar and wind resources, you can use it to [power a fuel cell](http://www.fchea.org/fuelcells) to generate electricity.

You can also use excess solar power (remember the oversupply problem?) to electrolyse water and generate hydrogen, and then store it for times when renewable resources are running low.

> Note that this production method described is for "green" hydrogen. "Blue" hydrogen, produced from carbon capture at fossil fuel plants, and promoted by oil and gas companies, can have [worse greenhouse gas emissions](https://cleantechnica.com/2021/08/12/theory-versus-reality-the-dirty-hydrogen-story/) than producing the equivalent energy from just natural gas, or even coal, in some cases.  The chart here illustrates this problem alongside "gray hydrogen," which skips the carbon capture part.

Hydrogen is now a major commercial interest and [governments around the world have instituted support policies](https://www.nrdc.org/experts/shruti-shukla/hydrogen-targeted-decarbonization-tool-not-panacea) toward hydrogen deployment. U.S. IRA incentives could make [U.S. green hydrogen the cheapest in the world](https://www.rechargenews.com/energy-transition/us-green-h2-would-become-worlds-cheapest-under-tax-credit-plan-in-new-manchin-approved-bill/2-1-1268259), although this industry—and related policies—are fast-changing.

While long-term energy storage and seasonal balancing of the grid [may be its most promising application](https://noahpinion.substack.com/p/i-come-bringing-good-news-about-hydrogen), hydrogen can also play a role in decarbonizing “hard-to-decarbonize” [industries like ammonia and steel](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S2214629621003017), [shipping](https://www.iea.org/articles/hydrogen-as-a-driver-for-the-decarbonisation-of-shipping) and [aviation](https://www.airbus.com/en/innovation/zero-emission-journey/hydrogen), and possibly [long-distance freight transport](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/operations/our-insights/global-infrastructure-initiative/voices/unlocking-hydrogens-power-for-long-haul-freight-transport). Hydrogen is [not competitive or well-suited](https://twitter.com/janrosenow/status/1409789732175945729?s=20&t=KF7hlKiPFBgCEERMMhjWMg) for heating buildings or most forms of ground transport. 

Note that hydrogen is a potent greenhouse gas and [emissions and leaks could undermine its climate benefits.](https://www.edf.org/media/study-emissions-hydrogen-could-undermine-its-climate-benefits-warming-effects-are-two-six) And while [global trade in hydrogen](https://hydrogencouncil.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/10/Global-Hydrogen-Flows.pdf) may develop, “[shipping liquid hydrogen is not going to be a thing.](https://twitter.com/MLiebreich/status/1596449504194367488?s=20&t=EP0iRQQ3aur6GNfQm2ugnw)”
