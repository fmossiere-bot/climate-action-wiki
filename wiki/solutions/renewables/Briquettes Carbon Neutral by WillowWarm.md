## SOCIAL Search Perplexity
People generally speak positively about WillowWarm briquettes, highlighting their high heat efficiency, long burn time, clean burn, and ease of use. Customers appreciate the reduced ash residue and the pleasant smell, noting it as a cost-effective and environmentally friendly alternative to traditional solid fuels[1][2].

> Regarding sustainability claims, WillowWarm briquettes are made from 100% renewable, Irish-grown willow and biomass, **without additives or binders**. The willow used is a short rotation coppice crop harvested every two years from plantations that can produce for about 36 years without replanting, which supports sustainable land use and carbon sequestration[3][4]. The company sources wood from FSC-certified forests, ensuring responsible forest management that preserves biodiversity and complies with strict environmental standards[5].

The briquettes have a low moisture content (less than 10%) and low ash content (less than 3%), contributing to a clean and efficient burn that meets Ireland and UK air quality standards for domestic solid fuels[2][5]. The combustion of these briquettes is considered carbon neutral because the CO2 released during burning is offset by the CO2 absorbed by the growing willow crops through photosynthesis, making them a sustainable fuel option[4].

In summary, the sustainability claims of WillowWarm briquettes are well-supported by their production methods, certifications, and customer feedback, making them a credible carbon-neutral and environmentally friendly heating fuel choice in Ireland[1][2][3][4][5].
