### Introduction

In light of escalating environmental worries and energy expenses, embracing renewable energy is no longer merely a choice but a crucial step. This guide serves as your roadmap to a greener and more affordable energy home. It aims to empower you with the knowledge and resources to explore your options, create a financial plan, and select a renewable energy system for your home.

If your home uses more energy than the typical home in your country, there are likely ways to reduce your energy consumption and expenses significantly. These changes can contribute to eliminating the carbon emissions associated with your home's energy system.

On average, households consume the following amounts of electricity annually:

US - 11,267 KWh per year

Spain - 4,928 KWh per year

UK - 4,266 KWh per year

### Understanding Electricity Needs

Before diving into renewable energy for your home, start by calculating your electricity needs. This helps figure out the size and cost of the system you need, how your energy use varies, and ways to reduce consumption. 

Analyze the daily use and wattage of all electrical devices. To calculate total consumption, multiply each appliance's wattage by its daily usage hours, adjusting for seasonal changes. Remember, improving your home's energy efficiency first can lead to needing a smaller, cheaper renewable energy system.

  

### How much could you save?

Starting with simple steps to save energy at home can lead to significant savings on your bills and contribute to environmental conservation. Actions like switching off appliances from standby mode, draught-proofing your home, and being mindful of your water and electricity use not only reduce your energy consumption but also your carbon footprint. Each small change, from taking shorter showers to filling your dishwasher completely before running it, adds up to create a bigger impact.

## Section 2: Exploring Renewable Energy Options

  

Planning a renewable energy system for your home involves understanding your current electricity usage, local regulations, and whether you'll connect to the grid or go off-grid. Options include solar, wind, microhydropower, and hybrid systems. While setting up can be costly and requires maintenance, it offers the satisfaction of energy independence and contributing to environmental conservation.

  

### Renewable Energy Options

When selecting a small renewable electric system for your home, understanding the basics is key. This includes knowing the available renewable energy resources, the economics and costs involved, where to place the system, how big it should be, relevant codes and regulations, and what installation and maintenance will be needed. These technologies can work alone, together, or alongside fossil fuels, creating hybrid systems.

Small Solar Electric Systems: Offer reliable, pollution-free electricity, especially in remote areas where traditional power lines are impractical.

Small Wind Electric Systems: Cost-effective for home use, with applications extending beyond electricity generation to tasks like water pumping.

Microhydropower Systems: Capable of generating significant electricity, enough to power large homes or small communities, depending on the system size.

Small Hybrid Systems: Combining solar and wind can provide a more consistent energy supply, capitalizing on the strengths of both sources.

Each option has its unique benefits, making renewable energy a versatile choice for powering homes.

### Solar Panels

Solar panels convert sunlight into electricity using cells, typically made of silicon, which work even on cloudy days. A standard home setup has about 10 panels, producing electricity for household use or for export. Before installation, consider your space and any required permissions. Solar panels can reduce electricity bills and carbon emissions, saving approximately one tonne of carbon annually in the UK. The initial cost for a 3.5kWp system is around £7,000, but savings can be made through the Smart Export Guarantee for exported electricity.[[x](https://energysavingtrust.org.uk/advice/solar-panels/)]

  

### Wind Turbines

  

Wind turbines, available as pole-mounted (6kW) and building-mounted (2kW), convert wind into electricity. The initial setup cost for a pole-mounted system is around £35,000, potentially saving £510 annually on electricity and reducing CO2 emissions by 2,000kg. Surplus electricity can earn approximately £400 yearly through Smart Export Guarantee payments.

Maintenance costs range from £100 to £200 yearly, with turbines lasting over 20 years. Inverter replacements may cost £1,000 to £2,000, and off-grid systems might need battery replacements every 6-10 years.[[x](https://energysavingtrust.org.uk/advice/wind-turbines/)]

### Renewable Energy Incentives

In England, Scotland, and Wales, you can find [financial help](https://energysavingtrust.org.uk/energy-at-home/financial-support/grants-and-loans/) for home renewable technology installations. This includes:

- Boiler Upgrade Scheme: Encourages heat pump installations in England and Wales to promote greener energy.
    
- Smart Export Guarantee: Ensures payment for surplus renewable electricity sent back to the grid in England, Scotland, and Wales.
    

Scottish residents also have access to grants and interest-free loans for home renewables through the Home Energy Scotland Grant and Loan. Unfortunately, there's no current funding for these technologies in Northern Ireland. These initiatives aim to make adopting renewable energy sources more feasible and rewarding.

## Section 3: Weekly Action Plan

### Week 1: Energy Audit and Efficiency

#### Objective: Understand your energy usage and implement no-cost energy-saving measures.

- #### Days 1-2: Analyze Your Energy Bill. Use resources like the Home Energy Scotland video to understand your energy consumption patterns and identify areas for improvement.
    
- #### Days 3-4: Calculate Your Electricity Needs. List all electrical appliances, their wattage, and usage frequency. Focus on identifying energy-saving opportunities that require no upfront costs, such as adjusting appliance usage times.
    
- #### Days 5-7: Implement No-Cost Energy Savings. Actions like turning off standby appliances, optimizing water usage, and being mindful of heating controls can lead to immediate savings. Utilize the tips provided for quick wins in energy conservation.
    

### Week 2: Research Renewable Energy Options

#### Objective: Decide on the most suitable renewable energy solutions for your home.

- #### Days 1-3: Explore Renewable Energy Options. Investigate solar panels, wind turbines, and other renewable sources. Use the "Go Renewable" tool to assess which technologies are feasible for your home.
    
- #### Days 4-5: Understand the Basics of Your Chosen Renewable Systems. Consider the size, placement, local regulations, and whether you'll go off-grid or stay connected to the grid.
    
- #### Days 6-7: Review Case Studies and Real-Life Examples. The Green Homes Network can provide valuable insights into how others have successfully transitioned to renewable energy.
    

### Week 3: Financial Planning for Renewable Transition

#### Objective: Develop a detailed financial plan for your transition to renewable energy.

- #### Days 1-2: Assess Costs and Savings. Based on your chosen renewable energy solutions, estimate the initial setup costs and potential savings on energy bills.
    
- #### Days 3-4: Explore Incentives and Funding. Look into available financial incentives such as the Boiler Upgrade Scheme and Smart Export Guarantee. Check for regional grants and loans that could offset costs.
    
- #### Days 5-7: Create Your Savings Plan. Determine how much you need to save monthly towards the renewable energy transition. Consider reallocating savings from your no-cost energy efficiency measures to fund this project.
    

### Week 4: Finalizing Your Renewable Energy Plan

#### Objective: Finalize your plan for transitioning to renewable energy, including selecting providers and planning the project timeline.

- #### Days 1-3: Select Your Renewable Energy Providers. Use the Renewable Installer Finder to connect with trusted MCS certified installers. Get quotes and discuss your project to understand timelines and logistics.
    
- #### Days 4-5: Review and Adjust Your Financial Plan. With quotes in hand, revisit your financial plan. Ensure your savings plan aligns with the project costs and timeline.
    
- #### Days 6-7: Plan for Next Steps. Schedule initial consultations or site assessments with your chosen installers. Prepare a checklist of actions needed before installation begins, such as applying for incentives or adjusting your household budget to accommodate savings goals.
    

#### By the end of these four weeks, you'll have a comprehensive understanding of your current energy use, identified cost-free efficiency improvements, selected the most suitable renewable energy solutions, and developed a financial plan to make the transition feasible.
