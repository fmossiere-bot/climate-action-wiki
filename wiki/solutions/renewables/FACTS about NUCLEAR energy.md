## Nuclear Capacity
As of 2023, the global capacity of operable nuclear power is approximately 391 gigawatts (GWe). Looking ahead to 2040, there are different projections for the expansion of nuclear power capacity based on various scenarios. In the Reference Scenario, which is informed by current government and utility targets, the projected nuclear capacity is expected to reach 686 GWe by 2040. In the Upper Scenario, which reflects more favorable conditions and the targets set by many countries to achieve net-zero carbon emissions, the capacity could reach as high as 931 GWe. Conversely, in the Lower Scenario, which assumes delays in implementing current plans, the capacity is estimated to be around 486 GWe by 2040.



These projections show a significant potential increase in nuclear power capacity over the next two decades, driven by the global effort to decarbonize energy supplies and ensure energy security, as well as the growing interest in deploying small modular reactors (SMRs) and microreactors. These developments are seen as key to achieving global net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2050 and providing reliable, secure access to clean energy.

# Does Nuclear Pollute? 
**Reference**: [Energy.gov](https://www.energy.gov/ne/articles/3-reasons-why-nuclear-clean-and-sustainable#:~:text=Nuclear%20is%20a%20zero%2Demission,byproducts%20emitted%20by%20fossil%20fuels.)

> Nuclear is a **zero-emission clean energy source**. It generates power through fission, which is the [process of splitting uranium atoms](https://www.energy.gov/ne/articles/fission-and-fusion-what-difference)to produce energy. The heat released by fission is used to create steam that spins a turbine to generate electricity without the harmful byproducts emitted by fossil fuels.

1. Nuclear energy protects air quality
2. Nuclear energy land footprint is small
3. Nuclear energy produces minimal waste
