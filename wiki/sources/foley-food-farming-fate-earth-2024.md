# Source: How Food and Farming Will Determine the Fate of Planet Earth

**Original source:** Project Drawdown, Jonathan Foley PhD, 30 March 2024
**URL:** https://drawdown.org/insights/how-food-and-farming-will-determine-the-fate-of-planet-earth

## What this source covers

A long-form essay by Jonathan Foley, Executive Director of Project Drawdown, arguing that agriculture has disrupted the planet more than any other human activity — including burning fossil fuels. The piece covers agriculture's land footprint, biodiversity impacts, freshwater use, chemical pollution, and contribution to climate change, before pointing toward Project Drawdown's food systems work as a path forward.

## Key claims

- Croplands (16 million km²) and pasture (34 million km²) together cover **37% of Earth's land surface** — more than Asia and Europe combined.
- **75% of all agricultural land** is used to graze animals or grow animal feed, making meat and dairy the single biggest driver of food's geographic footprint.
- Agriculture is responsible for **70% of global freshwater use** (irrigating crops), consuming ~2,800 km³/year.
- Fertiliser use has **more than doubled** the normal background flows of nitrogen and phosphorus across Earth's surface, creating "dead zones" in coastal waters.
- Agriculture and associated land use **directly cause ~22% of global greenhouse gas emissions**; the full food system (transport, packaging, waste) accounts for **~one-third** of all emissions.
- Deforestation for agriculture (pasture, soybeans, oil palm) accounts for **~10–11% of global GHG emissions**, equivalent to the entire US economy.
