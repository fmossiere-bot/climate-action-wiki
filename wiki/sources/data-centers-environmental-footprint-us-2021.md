# Source: The Environmental Footprint of Data Centers in the United States

**Original source:** Environmental Research Letters, Md Abu Bakar Siddik, Arman Shehabi, Landon Marston. Published 21 May 2021.
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1088/1748-9326/abfba1

## What this source covers

First sub-national study to estimate both the water footprint and carbon footprint of US data centres at the facility level, using spatially detailed records connecting each data centre to its specific power plants, water utilities, and wastewater treatment plants. Data is for 2018.

## Key claims

- US data centres accounted for **~1.8% of US electricity use** and **~0.5% of total US GHG emissions** in 2018.
- Total annual operational water footprint of US data centres in 2018: **5.13 × 10⁸ m³**.
- **Three-quarters of the water footprint** is indirect (via electricity generation), not direct cooling water.
- **70% of the water scarcity footprint** is attributed to data centres in the West and Southwest US — regions hosting ~20% of servers but drawing heavily on water-stressed watersheds.
- Hyperscale data centres are ~6× more water-efficient per workload than internal/colocation centres (2.10 vs 12.15 m³/workload).
- Carbon intensity of a data centre varies from **0.02 to 1 ton CO₂-eq/MWh** depending on location — a ~50× range.
- Strategically relocating future data centres to environmentally favourable regions could reduce the industry's water scarcity footprint by up to **90%** and carbon footprint by up to **55%**.
