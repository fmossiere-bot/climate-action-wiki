**Source:** RTÉ News, Pat McGrath, 17 June 2026 — https://www.rte.ie/news/environment/2026/0617/1578808-water-quality-report/

**What it covers:** Annual EPA water quality indicators report for 2025, covering rivers, lakes, estuaries, and coastal waters.

**Key findings:**
- No major improvements to water quality achieved in 2025; standards remain unsatisfactory in many areas
- Excess nutrients (phosphorous and nitrogen from agriculture and wastewater) are the biggest challenge
- 43% of rivers have nitrate concentrations that are too high — problem especially pronounced in east and southeast
- Over 30% of lakes have excess phosphorous — particularly acute in the border region
- Just over half of Ireland's rivers and lakes are rated in good or better biological quality
- More rivers declined in quality class than improved in 2025
- EPA calls for targeted, water-body-specific actions to accelerate improvement
- Local success: six of 16 rivers in Ballyteigue Bannow catchment (Co Wexford) improved in 2025
