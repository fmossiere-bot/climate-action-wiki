# Swiss glaciers facing 'enormous' loss from heatwave

**Source:** RTÉ News / AFP, 27 June 2026
**URL:** https://www.rte.ie/news/2026/0627/1580624-glaciers-heatwave/

## What it is

A short news report on the impact of Europe's June 2026 heatwave on Swiss glaciers. The piece centres on **Matthias Huss, head of GLAMOS (Glacier Monitoring in Switzerland)**, who warns that the snow accumulated during winter 2025–26 will have entirely melted by 30 June, the **second-earliest "glacier loss day" on record** after 2022.

## Key facts cited

- "Glacier loss day" expected around 30 June 2026 — three months earlier than a healthy year (mid-August on average)
- The only earlier loss day on record was 26 June 2022
- Rhone Glacier: 1 metre of vertical ice melt in just 10 days during the heatwave
- 25% less snow replenishing glaciers this winter compared with 2010–2020 average
- Saharan dust deposits in March accelerated melt (dust darkens ice, reducing albedo)
- Volume of Swiss glaciers shrank by 38% between 2000 and 2024
- Switzerland has already lost 1,200 glaciers in 50 years; only ~1,300 remain
- If warming continues at current rates, only small remnants of Swiss glaciers will exist by 2100

## Why it matters

The Swiss Alps feed two of Europe's major rivers — **the Rhine and the Rhone**. Once winter snow disappears from glacier surfaces, the darker bare ice underneath absorbs more solar radiation, creating an **accelerating melt feedback loop**. Huss frames the issue plainly: it is not single heatwaves but the cumulative effect of prolonged high temperatures that is destroying these ice masses.

## Tone and angle

Reportage-led, drawing heavily on a single expert source. The framing is matter-of-fact: this is the second-worst year in living memory, and the trajectory points to near-total loss within decades.
