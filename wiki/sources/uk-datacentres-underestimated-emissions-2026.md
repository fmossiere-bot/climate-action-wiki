## What it covers
The UK government revised its estimate of AI data centre carbon emissions upward by a factor of over 100, after a previous estimate was found to be a serious undercount. The revision was prompted by an investigation by Foxglove and Carbon Brief.

## Key facts
- Previous estimate: a maximum of 0.142 million tonnes CO₂ per year — since quietly deleted
- Revised estimate: 34–123 million tonnes CO₂ over 2025–2035 (0.9–3.4% of UK projected total)
- The lower end depends on AI model efficiency improvements and faster grid decarbonisation
- The revision was first reported by Politico and prompted by a Foxglove/Carbon Brief investigation
- Foxglove: the AI data centre buildout "could double the electricity consumption of the entire country" if unchecked
- The government declined to comment on record
