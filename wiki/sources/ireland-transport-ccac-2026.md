**Source:** RTÉ News, Aaron McElroy, 17 June 2026 — https://www.rte.ie/news/ireland/2026/0617/1578833-climate-change-review/

**What it covers:** The transport chapter of the Climate Change Advisory Council (CCAC) 2026 Annual Review.

**Key findings:**
- Ireland's dependence on fossil fuels in transport leaves it exposed to repeated fuel price shocks as global energy markets remain disrupted
- CCAC recommends accelerated investment in public transport, active travel, EV charging infrastructure and grid capacity
- Emergency responses to fuel price increases were not sufficiently targeted; supports should focus on those most exposed and least able to avoid fuel costs
- TFI network journeys up 6% in 2025 vs 2024; Local Link journeys up 19%; but total public transport journeys largely unchanged, suggesting near-full capacity at peak times
- Specific projects flagged: DART+ South West, Luas Finglas, NTA Park and Ride Investment Programme
- CCAC welcomes pilot EV scrappage scheme but calls for measures to increase EV uptake among lower-income households in car-dependent areas
- CCAC Chair Alex White: "Fossil fuel shocks are not one-off events; giving alternatives is the way to reduce exposure"
