# 'Severe' stress on oceans — UN World Ocean Assessment III (2026)

**Source:** The Guardian, Karen McVeigh, 8 June 2026 — https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2026/jun/08/un-world-ocean-assessment-severe-stress-sea-level-rise-doubles-pollution-fishing-climate

## Summary

The UN's third World Ocean Assessment, drawing on nearly 600 scientists from 86 countries, found the oceans are under "severe and accelerating" pressure. Sea level rise has doubled in rate: from 2mm/year before 2015 to 4.3mm/year by 2023. Sixteen per cent of the total increase in ocean heat since 1955 occurred after 2018 alone. The report, published on World Ocean Day 2026, covers the period 2021–2025.

## Key facts

- Sea level rise rate doubled: 2mm/year (pre-2015) to 4.3mm/year (2023).
- 16% of all ocean heat gain since 1955 happened after 2018.
- Oceans have absorbed 90% of excess atmospheric heat and 30% of CO₂ from fossil fuels.
- 52.1 million tonnes of plastic enter the ocean annually, contributing to 24.4 trillion microplastic particles.
- Microplastics affect more than 4,000 marine species.
- Only 27% of the ocean floor has been mapped by 2025.
- The high seas treaty came into force in 2026, applying protections to the two-thirds of ocean outside national jurisdiction.
- More than a third of the world's population lives within 100km of coasts; 11% live on land less than 10m above sea level.
- Ocean governance remains "fragmented" across sectors and regions.
