**Source:** Irish Independent, John Burns, 26 June 2026 — https://www.independent.ie/irish-news/data-centre-boom-sees-107-facilities-now-operating-in-ireland-with-18.5bn-invested/a/157776762.html

**What it covers:** Latest Bitpower analysis of Ireland's data centre sector, presented alongside updates on grid policy and planning.

**Key findings:**
- 107 data centres now operating in Ireland across 32 operators; 9 under construction; 43 with planning permission (total potential: ~160)
- Combined IT load of operational facilities: 1.3 GW; hyperscale operators (AWS, Microsoft, Google, Meta) account for ~75% of total computing capacity
- Total investment to date: €18.5bn
- EirGrid median-growth scenario: data centres will use 31% of all national electricity by 2034 (up from 22% in 2024)
- New CRU rules (moratorium lifted December 2025): new data centres must be energy neutral after six years and source 80% of electricity from renewables
- Three environmental groups granted High Court permission to challenge the new rules, arguing fossil fuels are still allowed for the first six years
- Dublin area still constrained for connections even under new rules
- AI hardware imports from Taiwan quadrupled in six months: €0.5bn (Q3 2025) to €2.2bn (Q1 2026)
- No official census of data centres in Ireland exists
