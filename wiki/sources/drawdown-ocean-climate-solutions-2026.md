# What floats and what sinks — Ocean-based climate solutions (Project Drawdown, 2026)

**Source:** Project Drawdown, Christina Richardson PhD, 2 June 2026 — https://drawdown.org/insights/what-floats-and-what-sinks-when-it-comes-to-ocean-based-climate-solutions

## Summary

A Project Drawdown research fellow reviewed 14+ ocean-based climate solutions and ranked them by readiness and climate impact. Offshore wind is far ahead as the top ocean solution. Protecting coastal wetlands (mangroves, salt marshes, seagrasses) and seaweed ecosystems is worthwhile despite modest scale. Novel marine carbon dioxide removal (mCDR) approaches remain highly uncertain. Ocean biomass sinking, artificial upwelling, and ocean fertilisation are not recommended.

## Key facts

- Offshore wind: estimated achievable impact of 1.90–3.04 Gt CO₂-eq/year. Top ocean-based solution.
- Protecting coastal wetlands + seaweed ecosystems: 0.21–0.30 Gt CO₂-eq/year combined.
- All other ocean actions (restoring ecosystems, reducing overfishing, protecting seafloor, improving aquaculture) deliver under 0.1 Gt CO₂-eq/year each.
- Ocean alkalinity enhancement and ocean electrochemistry: uncertain, still in R&D.
- Ocean biomass sinking, artificial upwelling, ocean fertilisation: not recommended — likely ineffective or ecologically risky.
- Ocean protection funding: ~$1.2 billion/year vs estimated need of ~$16 billion/year to achieve 30x30 target.
- The highest-impact climate actions available today are mostly on land: utility-scale solar, onshore wind, diet change, forest protection.
