**Source:** BBC Sport, Andrew Benson, 17 June 2026 — https://www.bbc.com/sport/formula1/articles/cvgjp4rj2p5o

**What it covers:** F1's annual sustainability review for the 2025 season, reporting progress against its Net Zero by 2030 commitment.

**Key findings:**
- Total CO₂ emissions fell from 228,793 tonnes (2018) to 148,805 tonnes (2025) — a 35% reduction; 12% reduction year on year
- Factory and facilities emissions down 64% vs 2018; logistics emissions down 21% year on year, 29% vs 2018
- Main levers: shift from air to sea freight, sustainable aviation fuel (SAF) investment, calendar geographic clustering
- From 2026, F1 mandates sustainable fuels in races — producing 80% fewer GHG emissions than fossil-fuel comparators
- Calendar reshuffles (e.g. Japan in spring grouped with Australia and China) cut travel emissions by ~3% for some sequences
- Net Zero by 2030 commitment = minimum 50% absolute emissions cut vs 2018; remaining unavoidable emissions offset via "credible programmes"
- Third-party reporting platform used for data calculation and transparency
