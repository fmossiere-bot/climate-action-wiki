## What it covers
A report commissioned by Friends of the Earth Ireland and Beyond Fossil Fuels quantifies the cost to Irish households of data centre electricity demand. It finds that the industry's high, inflexible demand has pushed up gas-set electricity prices, adding hundreds of euros to household bills over a decade.

## Key facts
- Ireland's data centres used 22% of national electricity in 2025 — more than all urban homes combined (CSO)
- Data centres "drained" €715m from the Irish economy and increased household bills by a cumulative average of €360 over 2015–2023
- Future exposure: €295–€644 cumulative per household from 2025–2034, or €633m–€1.43bn nationally
- The mechanism: data centres increase hours in which gas sets the electricity market price
- Ireland's requirement: new data centres must source 80% of energy from additional renewable capacity — the strictest rule in Europe
- Industry disputed findings, citing €18bn in investment and proportional network charge payments
- Beyond Fossil Fuels says the Irish case is "a warning for Europe"
