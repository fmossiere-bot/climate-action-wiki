# Source: SBTi Sets New Rules for Corporate Net Zero

**Publication:** Wall Street Journal (WSJ Pro Sustainable Business), Yusuf Khan, 11 June 2026
**URL:** https://www.wsj.com/pro/sustainable-business/climate-standard-setter-sbti-sets-new-rules-for-companies-seeking-net-zero-43a38733

## Summary

The Science Based Targets initiative published its long-awaited update to the corporate net-zero standard. The new framework moves away from a strict in-supply-chain decarbonisation approach and allows market-based mitigation tools (environmental credits, SAF credits) and carbon removals as part of a company's net-zero strategy. The overarching shift is from a rigid rulebook to a "best-efforts, continuous improvement" framework.

## Key points

- Companies may now count purchased environmental credits (e.g. SAF credits, renewable energy certificates) in target calculations
- Carbon removal technologies (DAC, reforestation credits) allowed from 2035 for residual hard-to-abate emissions
- "Best-efforts framework": companies must deploy every lever within their control and be transparent about barriers
- PPAs (power purchase agreements) now count if supplying low-carbon electricity in the company's region; cross-regional allowed if structural barriers exist
- Hourly electricity matching: "preferable" but not mandated for target-setting; tech companies with significant electricity use must report their hourly matching share
- Physical decarbonisation remains the primary expectation; credits are not a substitute for action
- CEO David Kennedy framed the transition as "a continuous improvement journey"
