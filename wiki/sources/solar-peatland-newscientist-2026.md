**Source:** New Scientist, Alec Luhn, 8 June 2026 (updated 23 June 2026)  
**Peer-reviewed study:** Ecological Solutions and Evidence, Hanna Rae Martens et al. — DOI: 10.1002/2688-8319.70259  
**Institution:** University of Greifswald, Germany; Wattmanufactur solar company

**What it covers:** The first study examining biodiversity impacts of placing solar panels on rewetted peatland, at a 30-hectare site in northern Germany operated by Wattmanufactur.

**Key findings:**
- Solar park on rewetted peatland recorded more diverse bird species than two nearby hay fields: both wetland species (white wagtail, reed bunting, grey heron) and woodland species (tree pipit, Eurasian tree sparrow, buzzard, kestrel) were found, alongside the threatened meadow pipit
- Hay fields had only grassland species; the solar park's panels appeared to function like shrubs and small trees as perching habitat
- Peatlands are the largest terrestrial carbon store, holding twice as much carbon as all forests
- 95% of peatlands in Germany and 80% in the UK have been degraded by drainage for agriculture or peat extraction
- Drained peat: once drained, microbes break down the ancient carbon it holds, releasing CO2 for decades or centuries
- Critical distinction: some of the 165 solar farms on degraded (still-drained) peatlands in Germany are releasing more greenhouse gases than their clean electricity displaces — the carbon benefit only comes when peat is rewetted first
- UK: 2,500 km² of peatland restored — only one-tenth of what has been damaged
- Germany has barred solar farms on degraded peat from minimum guaranteed electricity prices since 2023, but developers are not always required to declare whether their installation is on drained peat
- Only five solar farms in Germany are currently on rewetted peatland
- Catherine Waite (Cambridge): "Peatland PV could be a really good way to help regenerate heavily degraded agricultural peat, but that doesn't mean it would also be a good thing to put it on really healthy peatlands elsewhere"
- More government incentives needed for peatland PV to scale; no income stream beyond electricity sales currently
