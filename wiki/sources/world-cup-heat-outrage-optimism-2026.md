# Extreme Heat Breaks — The Climate Story Behind the 2026 World Cup

**Source:** Outrage+Optimism podcast, June 2026

## Summary

The podcast discusses the 2026 Men's Football World Cup as a live demonstration of climate adaptation. For the first time in football history, every match will include mandatory three-minute hydration breaks in each half. A quarter of the 104 matches are projected to be played in conditions exceeding recommended heat safety limits. The hosts explore whether the hydration breaks should be explicitly called "extreme heat breaks" or "climate breaks" to help a global audience of billions understand the climate crisis in real time.

The podcast also covers the transport failures at US venues (NJ Transit surge-priced return tickets to MetLife Stadium to $150, from under $15), the boycott Trump's World Cup (BTWC) movement, and growing frustration that climate change indicators keep appearing but fail to trigger widespread mindset shifts.
