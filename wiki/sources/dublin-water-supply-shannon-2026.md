**Source:** RTÉ News, Juliette Gash, 24 June 2026 — https://www.rte.ie/news/2026/0624/1580174-shannon-dublin-water/

**What it covers:** Evidence given by Uisce Éireann to the Oireachtas Infrastructure Committee on the Water Supply Project — a scheme to divert water from the River Shannon to the Greater Dublin area.

**Key findings:**
- Water demand in Greater Dublin (plus parts of Wicklow, Meath and Kildare) is on a "knife edge" and cannot be met sustainably without intervention
- Uisce Éireann's Water Supply Project: diverts 2% of Shannon's annual flow from an existing reservoir at the Parteen Basin, piped east
- Project described as capable of supporting up to 50% of Ireland's population
- Planning application submitted to An Coimisiún Pleanála in December 2025
- 80% of landowners along the pipeline route have signed voluntary agreements with Uisce Éireann; CPO powers available for remainder
- Dublin network leakage reduced from 37% to 29% — work ongoing but questions raised about whether fixing leaks should come before new infrastructure
- Uisce Éireann says leakage reduction and new supply can proceed in parallel, not sequentially
- Lough Derg and Parteen Basin water levels and leisure uses (fishing, recreation) projected to be unaffected
