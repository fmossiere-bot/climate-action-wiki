# Coffeeangel: Brewing a Better Future with Biodiversity

**Source:** Coffeeangel website — https://coffeeangel.com/pages/coffeeangel-brewing-a-better-future-with-biodiversity

## Summary

Coffeeangel, an Irish speciality coffee company, has partnered with Dunsany Nature Reserve (Co. Meath) and cleantech firm Bionua to purchase Nature Capital Credits — a financial instrument that funds carbon sequestration and biodiversity restoration. The company commits 5% of its profits over five years to this programme. The page provides context on Ireland's biodiversity crisis, explains how Nature Capital Credits work, and describes the technology (satellite imaging, ground sensors, AI) used to verify impact.

## Key facts

- Coffeeangel commits 5% of profits for five years to Nature Capital Credits at Dunsany Nature Reserve.
- 7,153,104.64 kg of CO₂ captured and stored at Dunsany since 2024.
- Minimum target: 80,000 kg of carbon removed and stored in the first year.
- Each Nature Capital Unit = 1 tonne of carbon removed + a proportionate share of biodiversity gain.
- Verified under ISO 14064-1, 14064-2, and 14064-3.
- Bionua monitors using multispectral satellite imagery, ground sensors, and AI analysis.
- Ireland biodiversity context: 85% of protected habitats in unfavourable condition; farmland bird populations down 50% since the 1970s; 30% of semi-natural grasslands lost in the last decade; 1 in 5 assessed species threatened; 1 in 3 bee species threatened.
- Dunsany Nature Reserve is 35 km from Dublin.
