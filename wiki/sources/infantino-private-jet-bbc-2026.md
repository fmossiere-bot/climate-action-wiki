**Source:** BBC Verify / BBC Sport, Jake Horton, Tom Shiel, Ciaran Varley and Katie Gornall, 28 June 2026 — https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/articles/cgev5wy0zg3o

**What it covers:** BBC Verify investigation tracking a private jet linked to FIFA and president Gianni Infantino across the 2026 World Cup group stage using FlightRadar24 data and published photographs.

**Key findings:**
- Infantino attended 24 matches in 16 days (11–27 June); private jet took 27 flights matching his confirmed attendance locations
- Total distance: at least 31,144 miles (50,122 km); more than 66 hours in the air
- Estimated emissions: ~516 tonnes CO₂e over the group stage — equivalent to 78 people's average annual greenhouse gas footprint
- Longest flight: Vancouver to Miami (2,800 miles / 4,507 km) on 13 June; shortest: Philadelphia to Teterboro (92 miles / 148 km)
- Aircraft believed to be a Gulfstream G650ER, fuel burn ~1,817 litres/hour
- Private jets are 5–14× more polluting than commercial planes and 50× more than trains (Denise Auclair, Transport & Environment)
- FIFA stated travel is "sometimes on commercial airlines, sometimes on private charter, depending on which is more efficient" — did not confirm plane type, passenger numbers, or whether offsets were used
- FIFA's own pledges: 50% emissions reduction by 2030, net zero by 2040
- SGR 2025 report: overall 2026 World Cup carbon footprint could reach 9 million tonnes CO₂e — almost double the average of past four World Cups
- Swiss regulator (2023): FIFA made "false statements" by calling Qatar 2022 "carbon neutral"
- Freddie Daley (Cool Down / Sussex University): Infantino's private jet use "symptomatic of FIFA's failings on the environment"
