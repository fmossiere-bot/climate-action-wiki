# Extreme weather in 2025 drove record wildfire emissions in Europe

**Source:** New Scientist, Alec Luhn, 29 April 2026 (updated 15 May 2026) — from `raw/papers/New Scientist 2.pdf`

## Summary

Europe is the world's fastest-warming continent, warming twice as fast as the global average since 1980. In 2025, it suffered unprecedented wildfires and heatwaves, including a three-week hot spell that reached 30°C inside the Arctic circle. European wildfires emitted 47 million tonnes of carbon in 2025, a record amount. Spain, the UK, the Netherlands, Germany, and Cyprus all broke their previous fire-emission records. Climate change made the extreme hot, dry, windy conditions in Portugal and Spain at least 40 times more likely.

## Key facts

- 2025 was the hottest year on record in the UK, Iceland, and Norway; one of the three hottest in Europe overall.
- More than 95% of Europe experienced above-average annual temperatures.
- Scandinavia, Finland, and north-western Russia suffered their worst-ever heatwave: 21 days of temperatures reaching 30°C at the Arctic circle.
- Climate change made the August wildfires in Portugal and Spain at least 40× more likely.
- Fires approached Madrid; Camino de Santiago pilgrimage route sections shut down; smoke reached the UK.
- Over 10,000 sq km burned in Portugal and Spain; at least 3 people killed.
- European wildfires emitted 47 million tonnes of carbon in 2025, a record.
- Soil conditions were the driest in 33 years of observations; over a third of Europe suffered extreme agricultural drought.
- The wet spring that boosted vegetation growth, followed by record summer heat that dried it out, created a tinderbox — a "volatile weather" pattern expected to worsen.
- The ocean around Europe broke the record for annual sea surface temperature for the fourth year running; 86% of those seas suffered strong, severe, or extreme marine heatwaves.
- Mediterranean Sea: 100% of its area affected by heatwaves for three consecutive years; waters in Italy and Spain reached 30°C.
- Solar generated a record 12.5% of Europe's electricity in 2025; total renewables share reached 46%.
