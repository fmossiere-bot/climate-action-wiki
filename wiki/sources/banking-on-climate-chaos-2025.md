# World's largest banks pledged $906bn to fossil fuel companies in 2025

**Source:** The Guardian, Oliver Milman, 9 June 2026 — https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2026/jun/09/world-banks-pledge-billions-fossil-fuel-industry-2025

## Summary

The annual Banking on Climate Chaos report (a coalition of environmental groups) found that the world's 65 largest banks committed $906 billion to the fossil fuel industry in 2025, up $64 billion (nearly 8%) on 2024. Since the Paris Agreement in 2015, these banks have funnelled a total of $8.7 trillion to fossil fuel production. The UN-backed Net-Zero Banking Alliance has been disbanded after high-profile departures.

## Key facts

- $906bn committed by 65 largest banks to fossil fuels in 2025; up 8% on 2024.
- $8.7 trillion in fossil fuel financing since the Paris Agreement (2015–2025).
- JPMorgan Chase: $58bn (up 13% from 2024) — world's leading fossil fuel financier.
- Top five: JPMorgan Chase, Bank of America, MUFG, Mizuho Financial, Citigroup. Barclays is highest-placed British bank (8th).
- "Dirty dozen" banks responsible for 40% of all fossil fuel funding.
- $508bn pledged for expansion of existing fossil fuel sites in 2025, up 27% on 2024.
- Three largest recipients: Venture Global, Enbridge, Energy Transfer (all US oil and gas).
- 26 of 65 banks reduced fossil fuel financing; BNP Paribas, UBS, La Caixa led reductions.
- Net-Zero Banking Alliance (UN-backed) disbanded after mass departures, many triggered by political pressure in the US.
- Almost all financing comes from six jurisdictions: US, Canada, Japan, China, UK, EU.
