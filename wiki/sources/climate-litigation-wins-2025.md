# Greenwashing, illegality and false claims: 13 climate litigation wins in 2025

**Source:** The Guardian, Isabella Kaminski, 31 December 2025 — https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2025/dec/31/greenwashing-illegality-false-claims-climate-litigation-wins-2025

## Summary

2025 marked ten years since the Urgenda case, when the Netherlands was first ordered by a court to cut its carbon emissions faster. The decade since has produced a wave of climate litigation globally. This Guardian article reviews 13 significant wins from 2025 alone, spanning greenwashing verdicts, illegal fossil fuel approvals, international court advisory opinions, and state-level climate plan litigation.

## The 13 wins

1. **Rosebank and Jackdaw ruled illegal (UK)** — A Scottish court ruled UK government approval of two North Sea oil and gas fields unlawful because it did not account for scope 3 (end-use) emissions from burning the extracted fuel.
2. **Brazil's largest coal plant scrapped** — Copelmi withdrew its Nova Seival plant and Guaíba mine plans, saying the project had become unfeasible after licences were suspended on climate grounds.
3. **German court opens door for climate damages (RWE case)** — A Peruvian farmer's decade-long case against RWE was rejected, but set a precedent on polluters' liability for emissions. Pakistani farmers subsequently launched a similar case.
4. **EnergyAustralia greenwashing settlement (Australia)** — Settled with Parents for Climate; company acknowledged carbon offsets do not undo GHG emissions and apologised to 400,000 customers. First such case in Australia.
5. **Inter-American court: human right to a stable climate** — Found states have a duty to protect the human right to a healthy climate.
6. **International Court of Justice advisory opinion** — Countries must prevent harm to the climate system; failure could result in compensation obligations.
7. **New South Wales coalmine expansion annulled** — Court blocked the Mount Pleasant Optimisation expansion for failing to account for scope 3 (export and burn) emissions.
8. **Apple banned from calling Watch "carbon neutral" (Germany)** — Frankfurt court ruled Apple could not substantiate the claim; Apple subsequently dropped the label in other markets too.
9. **Hawaii transport emissions roadmap** — State delivered a zero-emissions transport roadmap to 2045 as part of a settlement with youth climate litigants.
10. **Kenya Lamu coal plant cancelled** — Decade-long litigation ended with revocation of Amu Power's licence on environmental assessment grounds.
11. **TotalEnergies greenwashing conviction (France)** — Paris court ruled TotalEnergies's "reinvention" marketing campaign made misleading claims about net zero by 2050 while continuing fossil fuel production.
12. **JBS and Tyson meat greenwashing settlements (US)** — JBS paid $1.1m to New York; Tyson agreed to drop net zero claims after Environmental Working Group lawsuit.
13. **Three Norwegian oilfields ruled illegal** — Borgarting court of appeal declared licences for Breidablikk, Yggdrasil, and Tyrving unlawful for not accounting for full climate impacts.
14. **UK government forced to publish tougher climate plan** — Revised carbon budget and delivery plan published after original was ruled unlawful; commits to decarbonise electricity by 2030.
