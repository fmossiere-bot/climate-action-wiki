# Source: AMOC Monitoring Under Threat

**Publication:** The Guardian, 14 June 2026
**Authors:** Penny Holliday (National Oceanography Centre), Dr M Femke de Jong (NIOZ), Dr Sjoerd Groeskamp (NIOZ)
**URL:** https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2026/jun/14/amoc-collapse-europe-climate

## Summary

A commentary piece by three senior oceanographers arguing that AMOC monitoring is at acute risk of being defunded precisely when it is most needed. If the AMOC weakens severely or collapses, Europe could experience climate change up to 10 times faster than today.

## Key points

- AMOC collapse scenario: Europe could experience climate change **10× faster** than the current rate
- Systematic AMOC monitoring only began ~20 years ago, assembled from nationally funded research projects
- Trump administration budget cuts to NASA, NOAA, and NSF affect ~50% of total AMOC monitoring funding
- US also descoped the Ocean Observatories Initiative (announced June 2026)
- EU OceanEye initiative: €50m committed, but not yet operational
- Total cost of all AMOC monitoring: ~€25m/year (about 5 cents per EU citizen per year)
- New studies on whether AMOC has already weakened reflect genuine uncertainty, not scientific disagreement
- Call to EU, UK, and international partners to fund long-term, open-access AMOC monitoring
