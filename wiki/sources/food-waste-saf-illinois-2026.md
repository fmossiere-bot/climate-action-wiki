**Source:** TechXplore, Marianne Stein, 22 June 2026 — https://techxplore.com/news/2026-06-food-jet-fuel-simpler-refining.html  
**Peer-reviewed study:** Nature Sustainability, Buchun Si et al., 2026 — DOI: 10.1038/s41893-026-01848-1  
**Institution:** University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, Department of Agricultural and Biological Engineering

**What it covers:** A study outlining a simpler, lower-cost pathway to produce sustainable aviation fuel (SAF) from food waste, using hydrothermal liquefaction (HTL) and distillation-focused refining rather than heavy catalytic processing.

**Key findings:**
- Food waste converted to crude oil via HTL (mimics natural crude oil formation in a fraction of the time), then refined using distillation
- Simpler method than previous study — less catalytic intensity, lower cost — but produces lower-quality fuel that requires blending
- Tested as a 50-50 blend with conventional jet fuel; meets ASTM and FAA jet fuel standards
- Even 10-20% SAF blends are feasible and practical given supply constraints
- Biggest bottleneck: logistics of collecting and routing food waste from landfills and wastewater plants
- HTL works with treated wastewater as feedstock, enabling use of wet organic waste
- Byproduct (HTL aqueous phase, HTL-AP) is toxic but can be treated via electrochemical (EC) treatment to recover acids and nutrients
- EC treatment nearly triples cost per gallon vs baseline (higher capital/operating costs), but expected to fall with technology advances
- Lifecycle analysis: both baseline and improved EC treatment scenarios achieve negative carbon emissions
- Lead researcher: Prof Yuanhui Zhang (UIUC); compares approach to biodiesel blending model for cars
