**Source:** Visual Capitalist, Srijaa Chatterjee, 10 June 2026 — https://www.visualcapitalist.com/cp/europes-most-forested-countries/

**What it covers:** Ranking of European countries by forest cover as a percentage of land area, using World Bank data and the State of Europe's Forests 2025 report.

**Key findings:**
- Finland leads Europe at ~73% forest cover; Sweden close behind; Montenegro and Slovenia also in top tier
- Iceland, Ireland, and the UK have some of the lowest forest shares in Europe
- Northern dominance linked to boreal forest belt, colder climates, lower population density, and historically limited agriculture
- Low forest share in countries like Denmark and Netherlands linked to fertile lowland farmland devoted to agriculture
- Europe has generally expanded its forest area over the last three decades, unlike most other regions
- State of Europe's Forests 2025: forest area and biodiversity indicators trending upward, but growth rates slowing in some areas
- WRI highlights logging, natural disturbances, and climate-related impacts as increasing sources of forest loss
- EEA: forest resilience is becoming a critical policy issue as temperatures rise and extreme weather becomes more frequent
