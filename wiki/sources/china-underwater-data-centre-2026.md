## What it covers
China has switched on the world's first commercial underwater data centre (UDC), located off the coast of Shanghai in the Lin-gang Special Area. Powered primarily by offshore wind, it uses seawater as a heat sink instead of freshwater, and cuts land use by over 90% compared to conventional data centres.

## Key facts
- Built by a subsidiary of China Communications Construction; located off the coast of Shanghai
- 192 server racks across four levels; currently operating at 2.3 MW; planned capacity of 24 MW
- Uses a circulating copper-pipe heat exchange system that reduces electricity consumption by 22.8%
- Offshore wind farms estimated to generate 95% of the electricity needed
- Uses seawater as a coolant — no freshwater required
- Land use reduced by more than 90% vs above-ground facilities
- "For an undersea data center of the same scale, the electricity used for cooling would only account for about one-tenth of total power consumption" (Prof Li Zhen, Tsinghua University)
- 50 billion kWh/year electricity saving potential if scaled globally
- Open questions: ecological effects of releasing heat into local marine environments at commercial scale
