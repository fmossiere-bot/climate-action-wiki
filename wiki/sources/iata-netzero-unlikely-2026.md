# Airline industry chiefs say 2050 net zero goal now unlikely

**Source:** The Guardian, Gwyn Topham, 8 June 2026 — https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2026/jun/08/airline-industry-chiefs-willie-walsh-2050-net-zero-unlikely

## Summary

IATA director general Willie Walsh, speaking at the annual IATA summit in Rio de Janeiro, admitted that the aviation industry's collective pledge to reach net zero by 2050 will "probably not now be achieved." He blamed fuel suppliers, governments, and aircraft manufacturers for the likely failure and called for a "realistic timeline" to be established.

## Key facts

- SAF annual production will reach only 2.4 million tonnes in 2026 — just 0.8% of airline fuel needs. The 2050 target is 65%, or 500 million tonnes.
- There is "no path" to meet the ICAO target of a 5% emission reduction by 2030 via SAF.
- Walsh said CORSIA, the UN's aviation carbon offsetting scheme, is being "undermined" by government inaction.
- Aircraft manufacturers have delayed delivery of more fuel-efficient planes; air traffic management reforms have not materialised.
- UK just met its 2% SAF minimum in 2025, mostly from recycled cooking oil imported from Asia — not next-generation e-SAF.
- IATA sustainability VP called EU and UK e-SAF targets for 2030 "utterly detached from reality."
- Environmental campaigners have long viewed aviation's net zero pledges as greenwashing.
