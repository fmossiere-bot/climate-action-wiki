**Source:** The Guardian (comment), Jonathan Freedland, 26 June 2026 — https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2026/jun/26/climate-sceptics-record-temperatures-heatwave-ed-miliband-net-zero

**What it covers:** Opinion column using the June 2026 European heatwave as a frame to examine the "Don't Look Up" dynamic — political denial and distraction even as climate impacts become physically inescapable.

**Key factual content:**
- UK issued red heat warnings for three consecutive days in June 2026 — a first in Met Office history
- Temperatures exceeded 35°C and met the technical definition of "tropical" overnight
- Active wildfire burning in Derbyshire — extraordinary for a temperate country in June
- European heatwave: Germany and Poland bracing for 40°C
- "Anti-woke Davos" conference (Alliance for Responsible Citizenship) in London, backed by GB News owner and fossil fuel companies; attendees included Trump's energy secretary Chris Wright (ex-fossil fuel executive)
- Attendees disparaged UK net zero policies and Ed Miliband while reportedly sweltering inside in 35°C+ heat
- Chris Wright described UK green policies as a "tragic mistake"
- Political context: net zero also criticised by Kemi Badenoch, some trade unions, Tony Blair, and Mark Carney (who dismantled multiple Canadian green measures)
- European Commission also described as "easing the pressure" on environmental policy
- Trump-Iran link: Trump's capitulation on Iran deal in June 2026 partly driven by desire to lower petrol prices — illustrating how fossil fuel dependence shapes geopolitics
- Column argues that the response pattern — distraction, displacement, denial even while living through climate impacts — mirrors the "Don't Look Up" dynamic
