**Source:** New Scientist, Karmela Padavic-Callaghan, 12 June 2026 (updated 24 June 2026)

**What it covers:** The first experiment in quantum cryptocurrency mining, using a D-Wave Advantage2 quantum computer on the Quip experimental blockchain, set up by Colton Dillion at Postquant Labs.

**Key findings:**
- Quip blockchain uses an optimisation-type proof-of-work problem (e.g. scheduling, portfolio selection) — hard for classical devices but tractable for quantum machines
- D-Wave Advantage2 is available to Quip for ~5 minutes/day; in that window it wins 92% of blocks it competes for
- Energy use: Advantage2 uses ~12.5 watts vs ~1,334 watts for a conventional machine doing the same task — approximately 100× less power
- A conventional computer capable of beating Advantage2 at this task would need ~300× the power of the quantum machine
- Quip is designed to be quantum-safe — unlike many existing blockchains, which may need software updates to resist quantum attack
- Caution from Olivier Ezratty (Quantum Energy Initiative): capital and manufacturing costs of quantum hardware make purely economic comparisons difficult at scale
- Optimism from Carlos Perez-Delgado (University of Kent): "quantum technologies have a real opportunity to have a huge impact"
- Longer-term goal: Quip as a distributed worldwide quantum computer network, democratising access to rare quantum hardware
- Other firms working on quantum proof-of-work: BTQ Technologies, Quandela (photonic quantum computers)
