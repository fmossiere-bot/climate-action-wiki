**Source:** New Scientist, Alec Luhn, 11 June 2026 — https://appuk.newscientist.com/2026/06/11/2530049/content.html  
**Peer-reviewed study:** Science, DOI: 10.1126/science.aee5790 — co-authored by Ilissa Ocko (Spark Climate Solutions)

**What it covers:** Research calling for indirect greenhouse gases to be included in national climate targets and Paris Agreement action plans.

**Key findings:**
- ~15% of total warming since pre-industrial times caused by indirect greenhouse gases (mainly CO and VOCs) — double the contribution of nitrous oxide
- Carbon monoxide + VOCs + black carbon together have caused ~0.3°C of warming
- Mechanism: CO and VOCs react in the atmosphere to form low-level ozone, which traps heat; they also consume hydroxyl radicals that would otherwise break down methane
- Indirect GHGs break down within hours to a few years (unlike CO₂ which lasts centuries) — so cutting them would quickly slow the rate of warming
- Few countries include these gases in emissions reduction targets; Paris Agreement action plans do not mention them
- Sources: incomplete combustion of fossil fuels (gas boilers, older vehicles), agricultural burning of grasslands and forests, paints and cleaning solvents
- Risk with hydrogen transition: hydrogen leakage and venting could add 0.1°C by 2100 if decarbonisation relies heavily on burning hydrogen instead of batteries
- Nitrogen oxides (NOx) have a complex dual role: net cooling overall, but locally warm by producing ozone
- Key researchers: Ilissa Ocko (Spark Climate Solutions), Alex Archibald (Cambridge), Alastair Lewis (York)
