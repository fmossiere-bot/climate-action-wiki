## What it covers
More than 100 new UK data centres have requested gas network connections, in some cases as permanent primary power supply rather than backup, because they cannot access the electricity grid fast enough. This risks derailing the UK's Clean Power 2030 target.

## Key facts
- 100 GW of data centre projects are queued to connect to the UK's National Grid (Ofgem)
- Over 100 gas connection requests received from data centre operators in two years (Future Energy Networks)
- These requests amount to more than 15 TWh of gas energy per year — enough to power London for ~4.5 months
- Some developers are requesting over 100 MW of gas power on a permanent basis
- UK's Clean Power 2030 target aims for less than 5% unabated gas in the electricity system
- US parallel: 11 data centres built for Meta, OpenAI, Microsoft and xAI will reportedly emit more CO₂ than Morocco
