# Source: Welcome to the Most Polluting Games Ever

**Publication:** We Don't Have Time, Markus Lutteman, June 2026
**URL:** https://app.wedonthavetime.org/posts/44991735-7f37-43f6-bca2-c677af540e95

## Summary

An analysis of the environmental contradictions of the 2026 FIFA World Cup. Despite sustainability claims on its website, FIFA expanded the tournament to 48 teams and 104 matches across 16 cities on a continent, signed a four-year deal with Saudi Aramco, and is hosting the event in a country that has withdrawn from the Paris Agreement.

## Key points

- University of Manchester research: 2026 World Cup could be the most polluting in tournament history
- Expanded format forces fan itineraries of thousands of kilometres (e.g. Bosnia: Toronto, LA, Seattle = 5,000+ km before knockout rounds)
- Queen's University Belfast: 14 of 16 venues exceed safety thresholds for extreme heat, flooding, or heavy rain
- FIFA signed a 4-year deal with Saudi Aramco in 2024; Aramco CEO called phasing out oil "a fantasy" weeks before signing
- SGR (Scientists for Global Responsibility) research: Aramco sponsorship could induce an additional **30 million tonnes of CO₂e** in 2026
- Dr Oscar Berglund (Bristol): "FIFA has made elite men's football the primary target of petrostate sportswashing"
- 2034 World Cup awarded to Saudi Arabia, a country that repeatedly obstructs UN climate negotiations
- UK: growing number of local football matches cancelled due to extreme weather (flooding); Met Office projects winters 30% wetter by 2070 under high-emissions scenario
- FIFA revenue for 2023–2026 cycle: ~$13 billion — almost twice the previous cycle
