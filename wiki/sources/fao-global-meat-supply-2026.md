## What it covers
A report from the UN Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO) documenting a fourfold rise in global meat supply since 1961, with particular growth in poultry and pork. The article includes expert criticism that the report fails to recommend reducing meat consumption in wealthy countries despite clear evidence of climate harm.

## Key facts
- Average global meat supply rose from 25 kg per person in 1961 to 47 kg per person in 2022
- Poultry supply rose from below 3 kg per person to 17 kg; pork doubled to 15 kg; beef held steady at 9 kg
- Agriculture is the second most polluting economic sector; its emissions are forecast to rise 7.6% over the next decade
- Livestock is responsible for an estimated 80% of that increase
- About 14% of meat and milk is lost during production or wasted before consumption
- Animal agriculture is responsible for 12–20% of planet-heating emissions and is a leading cause of biodiversity loss
- The IPCC identifies a shift to plant-rich diets as one of the most effective demand-side climate actions
