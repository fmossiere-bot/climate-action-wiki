# Volatile summer weather threatens to turn World Cup into test of heat

**Source:** Reuters, Angelica Medina and Janina Rios, 10 June 2026 — https://www.reuters.com/business/environment/volatile-summer-weather-threatens-turn-world-cup-into-test-heat-2026-06-10/

## Summary

Climate Central research found climate change has increased the likelihood of performance-impairing temperatures at 97 of the 104 World Cup matches. The key measure used is wet-bulb globe temperature (WBGT), which combines heat, humidity, sunlight, and wind. High-humidity venues include Houston, Miami, Dallas, and Monterrey.

Key physiological detail: 75% of energy used during exercise is converted to heat, not movement. Humidity is the critical factor since sweat cools only when it evaporates. Scientists called for FIFA to mandate interventions at 26°C WBGT and to consider postponing matches at 28–30°C.

## Key facts

- Climate change raised performance-impairing heat risk at 97 of 104 matches.
- Biggest increase: Uruguay vs Spain in Guadalajara on 26 June, with a 70% chance of performance-impairing heat — 37 percentage points higher than without climate change.
- Nearly half of all matches face at least a 50% chance of temps exceeding 28°C, linked to declines in sprinting, distance covered, and recovery time.
- Twice the heat risk compared to the 1994 World Cup, held in the same country.
- Mexico City's altitude (2,240m) adds an additional performance factor.
- FIFA mandated three-minute hydration breaks in each half.
