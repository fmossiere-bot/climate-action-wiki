## What about Clouds? 
#clouds
Source: [New Scientist](https://www.newscientist.com/article/mg26335070-800-were-finally-solving-the-puzzle-of-how-clouds-will-affect-our-climate/?utm_campaign=RSS%7CNSNS&utm_source=NSNS&utm_medium=RSS&utm_content=currents) 
Kate Marvel mentioned it clearly in her talk, we don’t know everything and one thing that is still very unsure is the effect that Cloud will have/are having And create a wide range of prediction in Climate Models. 

> Will the world warm by a manageable 1.5°C or a hellish 4.5°C, given a doubling of carbon dioxide from pre-industrial levels

But that could change. This article from New Scientist details how we have been learning more about clouds and how they might impact Climate change

> The Cloud Experiment in the CERN is the first to be able to recreate cloud atmospheric condition, and this could have a massive impact as an input on CLimate Model which today have to use a wide range of prediction because the range of ipmact Clouds can have is very wide, from absorbing, reflecting a big part of the warming to trapping more heat from escaping Since there are more clouds today than before, this is a massive uncertainty that we need to figure out

Some resaearchers focus on Crystals in Clouds, while other recreate Cloud chambers,

> Just how much uncertainty was made clear in a landmark study by [Steven Sherwood](https://www.unsw.edu.au/staff/steven-sherwood) at the University of New South Wales in Australia and his colleagues. They [identified the six most important changes](https://agupubs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1029/2019RG000678) in clouds caused by global warming that will, in turn, influence climate change. These cloud feedbacks include less cloud cover overall, a shift of mid-latitude clouds towards the poles and more water in icy clouds (see [“The cloud forecast”, below](https://www.newscientist.com/article/mg26335070-800-were-finally-solving-the-puzzle-of-how-clouds-will-affect-our-climate/?utm_campaign=RSS%7CNSNS&utm_source=NSNS&utm_medium=RSS&utm_content=currents#DeepDive-1)). By their estimate, the total effect of the feedbacks ranged from no impact on the amount of warming to a substantial increase.


Aerosols also matter. We know they have an influence on the cloud formation itself, 
> From dust and the soot emitted by power plants to floating fungi, aerosols serve as seeds on which water droplets or ice crystals can form and create a cloud. “Clouds have a very, very difficult time forming in the absence of these particles,” says Feingold. The size, type and number of particles affect the brightness of clouds by changing the number of droplets or crystals in them.

Current climate models can represent how aerosols affect the brightness of clouds fairly well, but the models don’t do a great job of capturing the particles’ knock-on effects, such as changes to the lifetime of a cloud
Understanding how particules affect a cloud become more important as we reduce air pollution, and so the concentration of some of those particules will decrease We need to understand the knock-on effect this will have on clouds cloour, lifetime, formation at altitude etc …
