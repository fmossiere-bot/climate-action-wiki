# Global Consumption of Soft Pocket Tissues

The global tissue paper market is experiencing robust growth, driven by factors such as increasing hygiene awareness, urbanization, and rising disposable incomes. Within this market, soft pocket tissues hold a significant position, offering convenience and portability for consumers. This article explores the global consumption of soft pocket tissues, examining market trends, production and consumption patterns, and key drivers influencing demand.

This growth is attributed to several factors, including increased hygiene awareness among consumers, leading to higher demand for tissue paper products. Rapid urbanization, particularly in developing economies, further fuels this growth by increasing the demand for convenient and on-the-go hygiene products 2. However, price sensitivity among consumers can lead to price wars and margin pressures for manufacturers 3.

## Soft Pocket Tissues: Production and Consumption

While overall data on the global consumption of soft pocket tissues is limited, some sources provide specific figures and projections. North America and Europe are major consumers of these products. In North America, the emphasis on hygiene and the availability of numerous brands and products contribute to the high consumption of soft pocket tissues 2. Similarly, in Europe, where pocket packs are a leading product segment, convenience and portability drive demand 5.

Interestingly, there are notable differences in tissue consumption patterns within Europe. For instance, Western Europe has a significantly higher per capita tissue consumption (approximately 16 kg) compared to Eastern Europe (approximately 7 kg) 6. This highlights the influence of cultural factors and economic development on tissue consumption habits.

Asia-Pacific is also a significant market for soft pocket tissues, with production concentrated in countries like China 7. The rising disposable incomes and changing lifestyles in developing countries within the region are propelling the growth of this market 1. However, it's worth noting that China experienced surprisingly weak consumption growth in 2022, remaining below 2% despite expectations of higher growth 8. This unexpected trend underscores the dynamic nature of the market and the influence of various economic and social factors.

A unique aspect of soft pocket tissue consumption in Asia, particularly in Japan, is the practice of tissue pack marketing 9. Imagine walking down a busy street in Tokyo and being approached by someone offering a free pack of tissues. This isn't just an act of generosity; it's a widespread marketing strategy where businesses distribute tissues with advertisements printed on the packaging. This practice originated in Japan in the late 1960s as a replacement for promotional matchboxes and has become a ubiquitous part of the consumer landscape 9.

## Factors Driving Demand for Soft Pocket Tissues

Several factors contribute to the increasing demand for soft pocket tissues:

- Hygiene concerns: Soft pocket tissues offer a hygienic and disposable option for various needs, including nasal hygiene, wiping, and cleaning 10. They provide a convenient way to maintain personal hygiene and prevent the spread of germs, especially in public spaces or during travel.
    
- Convenience: Pocket tissues are compact and portable, making them ideal for on-the-go use 11. The rising popularity of pocket packs reflects this demand for convenience, as consumers increasingly seek products that fit seamlessly into their busy lifestyles 2.
    
- Increasing disposable incomes: Rising disposable incomes enable consumers to afford premium and value-added tissue products 2. This has led to a growing demand for tissues with enhanced features such as lotions, scents, and antibacterial properties 2. Consumers are also increasingly opting for eco-friendly and biodegradable options, reflecting a growing awareness of environmental issues 2.
    
- Innovation in Packaging: Tissue manufacturers are constantly innovating on packaging and product formats to attract consumer attention and convey quality 12. This includes introducing new packaging designs like cube box packaging, re-sealable anti-dust flaps on boxes, portable pop-up packs, and click-open lids. These innovations not only enhance convenience but also contribute to the overall appeal and perceived value of the product.
    
- Health Concerns: While soft pocket tissues are generally considered hygienic, there are potential health concerns associated with their use 13. Some tissues may contain chemicals that can cause skin irritation or respiratory issues, especially for those with sensitive skin or allergies. It's important for consumers to be aware of these potential risks and choose products that are safe and gentle.

The tissue paper market is dominated by key players such as Kimberly-Clark, Procter & Gamble, Essity, and Georgia-Pacific 14. ## Sustainability and Environmental Impact

The production and consumption of soft pocket tissues have an environmental impact. The manufacturing process involves the use of trees, water, and energy, and can contribute to deforestation, water pollution, and greenhouse gas emissions 18. Transportation of raw materials and finished products further adds to the environmental footprint.

However, there is a growing trend towards sustainability in the tissue paper industry. Consumers are increasingly aware of the environmental impact of their choices and are seeking eco-friendly alternatives. This has led to the development of tissues made from recycled materials, bamboo, and other sustainable sources.

For example, Tesco, a major supermarket chain in the UK, has taken a significant step towards sustainability by packaging its pocket tissues in recyclable paper 19. This initiative aims to reduce plastic waste and promote responsible consumption.

Interestingly, there is a significant generational divide in sustainability preferences 2. Gen Z and Gen X consumers are more likely to prioritize eco-friendly products and are willing to pay a premium for them, while Millennials show a lower inclination towards such options. This highlights the evolving consumer landscape and the need for manufacturers to cater to diverse preferences.

## Historical Growth and Future Projections

While specific historical data on the growth of the soft pocket tissues market is limited, the overall tissue paper market has experienced steady growth in recent years. The COVID-19 pandemic further accelerated this growth, as consumers prioritized hygiene and cleanliness 3. The pandemic also led to some initial challenges, including panic buying and temporary shortages of tissue paper products 3. However, the industry adapted to these challenges and continued to meet the growing demand.

Future projections indicate continued growth for the soft pocket tissues market, driven by factors such as increasing hygiene awareness, urbanization, and rising disposable incomes 2. The market is expected to witness further innovation in terms of product features, packaging, and sustainability.

## Conclusion

Soft pocket tissues have become an essential part of the global tissue paper market, offering convenience and hygiene to consumers worldwide. The market is driven by factors such as increasing hygiene awareness, urbanization, and rising disposable incomes. While North America, Europe, and Asia-Pacific are key markets for these products, consumption patterns and preferences vary across regions.

The industry is dominated by major manufacturers like Kimberly-Clark and Procter & Gamble, but smaller players and regional manufacturers also contribute to the market's diversity. The trend towards sustainability is gaining momentum, with consumers increasingly seeking eco-friendly alternatives and manufacturers responding with innovative solutions.

Looking ahead, the soft pocket tissues market is poised for continued growth, driven by evolving consumer preferences, increasing demand for convenience and hygiene, and a growing focus on sustainability. However, challenges such as price sensitivity and potential health concerns need to be addressed to ensure the market's long-term success.

# The impact of Plastic packaging

While precise figures on the environmental impact of plastic packaging for pocket tissues are not readily available, it's safe to say that it contributes significantly to plastic waste and pollution.

Here's why:

- Widespread Use: Pocket tissues are consumed globally, with billions of units sold each year . This translates to a massive amount of plastic packaging entering the waste stream.  
    
-   
    
- Single-Use Packaging: Most pocket tissues are packaged in single-use plastic wrappers or film, which are often discarded after a single use . This contributes to the growing problem of plastic pollution.  
    
-   
    
- Environmental Impact: Plastic waste from pocket tissue packaging can end up in landfills, where it takes hundreds of years to decompose . It can also pollute oceans and harm marine life .  
    
-   
    
- Health Concerns: Some plastic packaging may contain harmful chemicals like BPA and phthalates, which can leach into the environment and potentially pose health risks .  
    
-   
    

However, there's a growing trend towards sustainable alternatives:

- Paper Packaging: Some manufacturers are starting to use recyclable paper packaging for pocket tissues . This shift helps reduce plastic waste and promotes responsible consumption.  
    
-   
    
- Biodegradable Options: Companies are exploring biodegradable and compostable materials for tissue packaging, further reducing the environmental impact.
    

The move towards sustainable packaging for pocket tissues is crucial for minimizing plastic waste and protecting the environment. As consumers become more aware of the environmental impact of their choices, the demand for eco-friendly alternatives is likely to increase, driving further innovation in the tissue industry

  
  

# How could we make Pocket Tissues even more eco-friendly

To make pocket tissue paper as eco-friendly as possible, several key areas need to be addressed: the source of the fibers, the production process, and the packaging. Here's a breakdown:

1. Sustainable Fiber Sourcing:

- Bamboo:
    

- Bamboo is a rapidly renewable resource, growing much faster than trees.  
    
-   
    
- It requires fewer pesticides and fertilizers.  
    
-   
    
- Choosing bamboo from FSC-certified sources ensures responsible harvesting.  
    
-   
    

- Recycled Paper:
    

- Using post-consumer recycled paper significantly reduces the need for virgin fibers.
    
- This conserves forests and reduces waste.  
    
-   
    

- Other Alternative Fibers:
    

- Exploring other fast-growing, sustainable fibers like bagasse (sugarcane fiber) can further reduce environmental impact.  
    
-   
    

1. Eco-Friendly Production:

- Chlorine-Free Bleaching:
    

- Avoid traditional chlorine bleaching, which releases harmful dioxins.  
    
-   
    
- Opt for elemental chlorine-free (ECF) or totally chlorine-free (TCF) bleaching methods.
    

- Reduced Water and Energy Consumption:
    

- Implementing closed-loop water systems and energy-efficient processes minimizes environmental impact.  
    
-   
    

- Minimal Chemical Use:
    

- Using biodegradable and non-toxic chemicals in the production process is crucial.
    

1. Sustainable Packaging:

- Plastic-Free Packaging:
    

- Eliminating plastic packaging is essential.
    
- Using recycled cardboard or biodegradable materials for wrappers and boxes.
    

- Minimal Packaging:
    

- Reducing the amount of packaging material used minimizes waste.  
    
-   
    

- Recyclable Packaging:
    

- Ensuring that the packaging used is easily recycable.
    

Key Considerations:

- FSC Certification:
    

- The Forest Stewardship Council (FSC) certification ensures that the fibers come from responsibly managed forests.  
    
-   
    

- Life Cycle Assessment:
    

- Conducting a life cycle assessment can help identify and minimize the environmental impact of the entire product, from raw material sourcing to disposal.  
    
-   
    

- Compostability:
    

- While pocket tissues are not always ideal for home composting, exploring industrial compostability options can be beneficial.
    

By focusing on these areas, manufacturers can significantly reduce the environmental footprint of pocket tissue paper.
