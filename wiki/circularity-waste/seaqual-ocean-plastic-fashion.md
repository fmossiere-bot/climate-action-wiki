> **Editorial summary:** Seaqual is a Spanish-founded initiative that collects marine litter from fishing fleets and coastal communities worldwide and converts it into certified recycled textile materials for the fashion industry. This piece examines its full industrial structure: how plastic is recovered and processed, the precise blend ratios in its yarn products (10% marine plastic, 90% land-based rPET), the life cycle assessment data showing a 37% reduction in carbon emissions vs virgin polyester, and the end-of-life pathways including textile-to-textile chemical recycling. It is a detailed industry analysis rather than a general overview.

# Industrial Analysis of the Seaqual Initiative: Organizational Structure, Product Composition, Global Value Chain, and End-of-Life Circularity Pathways

## Organizational Architecture and Stakeholder Ecosystem

The Seaqual Initiative operates through a highly integrated organizational structure designed to address the systemic issue of marine plastic pollution. Founded in 2016 by a consortium consisting of Spanish textile spinners Antex and Textil Santanderina, alongside the eco-fashion brand Ecoalf, the initiative was conceived not as a traditional environmental charity, but as an industrial-scale intervention. The governing corporate entity is Seaqual 4U, S.L., a Madrid-registered commercial company in which Textil Santanderina holds a 50% direct ownership stake. This company, led by CEO François Devy and managing director Michel Chetpa, handles the global commercialization, licensing, and supply chain of upcycled materials.

Complementing this commercial engine is the Seaqual Foundation, which operates as the non-profit arm of the ecosystem. This hybrid structure allows the Seaqual Initiative to behave as a social enterprise or "Zebra company," a model that avoids the singular focus on profit maximization in favor of balancing economic viability with social and environmental regeneration.

The division of labor between these two entities ensures a comprehensive approach to environmental, social, and governance (ESG) principles. While Seaqual 4U, S.L. coordinates the industrial supply chain—securing contracts with waste management firms, spinners, and brand partners—the Seaqual Foundation drives local regenerative projects. These non-profit activities include supporting local coastal communities, funding education programs on plastic pollution, and leading biodiversity restoration projects, such as the rehabilitation of posidonia meadows, sea turtle protection, and papyrus wetland preservation.

To protect the integrity of its environmental claims, the Seaqual Initiative employs a strict licensing model. Any brand, fabric mill, or manufacturer wishing to purchase or utilize Seaqual materials must sign the "SEAQUAL Trademark & Copyright License Agreement". This agreement establishes a closed, audited B2B community where certified Seaqual materials can only be traded among licensed entities. By mandating that the entire supply chain remain certified, the initiative prevents unauthorized blending and ensures complete accountability from raw material recovery to the retail product.

|**Entity / Arm**|**Legal Structure**|**Primary Function**|**Sourcing & Financial Model**|**Key Operations & Interventions**|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**Seaqual 4U, S.L.**|For-profit Joint Venture (50% direct ownership by Textil Santanderina)|B2B commercialization, industrial scale-up, and IP licensing|Commercial licensing fees, transaction commissions, and direct sales of certified polymers|Brand integration, supply chain auditing, and technical spinning joint-ventures|
|**Seaqual Foundation**|Non-profit Registry|Public advocacy, corporate engagement, and environmental education|Philanthropic donations, corporate partnerships, and voluntary brand contributions|Coastal clean-ups, educational outreach, and marine biodiversity preservation projects|

## Material Sourcing, Geographic Coordinates, and Collection Protocols

The material sourcing strategy of the Seaqual Initiative relies on a global network of collection points located in areas with inadequate waste management infrastructure, primarily in Spain, Tunisia, Egypt, Morocco, Vietnam, Mexico, and Brazil. Sourcing strategies specifically target actual marine litter found in aquatic environments, oceans, beaches, rivers, and estuaries, rather than Ocean Bound Plastic (OBP), which is typically collected up to 50 kilometers inland.

The collection process leverages local fishermen, non-governmental organizations (NGOs), and community clean-up crews. Sourced materials undergo rigorous geographic sorting depending on their physical characteristics. For example, the Mediterranean Sea, particularly near Barcelona, serves as the primary collection point for discarded polyamide fishing gear.

|**Sourcing Location / Region**|**Primary Sourcing Partners**|**Dominant Material Stream**|**Collection Method**|**Physical Condition of Waste**|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**Iberian Peninsula / Mediterranean Sea (Spain)**|Eastern Spanish fishing fleets and maritime cooperatives|Polyamide 6 (PA6) fishing nets and maritime ropes|Direct retrieval by deep-sea fishing trawlers and harbor recovery programs|High physical degradation, heavy biological fouling, and high salt concentration|
|**North Africa (Tunisia, Egypt, Morocco)**|Local fishing cooperatives, municipal waste managers, and coastal NGOs|Polyethylene Terephthalate (PET) beverage bottles and rigid packaging|Manual river and coastal clean-ups, estuary skimming systems|High UV degradation, sand accumulation, and mechanical weathering|
|**Latin America (Mexico, Brazil)**|Local waste-picker unions and regional beach clean-up crews|Post-consumer PET bottles and consumer containers|Scheduled coastal sweeps and estuary barrier trash recovery|Medium physical weathering, high terrestrial contamination|
|**Southeast Asia (Vietnam)**|Local coastal collection hubs and fishing communities|Polyethylene Terephthalate (PET) and mixed low-density plastics|Estuary barriers, river mouth clean-ups, and coastal collection programs|High contamination, severe mechanical damage, bio-fouling|

Notably, the proportion of usable plastic recovered varies significantly by source. Beach cleanups yield a higher concentration of plastics, with PET beverage bottles representing up to 40% of the retrieved waste due to tidal accumulation. Conversely, waste retrieved from the ocean floor by fishing trawlers is highly heterogeneous, containing only 5% to 10% PET plastic, with the remainder consisting of metals, glass, rubber, and mixed organic debris. This material is highly degraded by prolonged exposure to ultraviolet (UV) radiation, saltwater salinity, and mechanical friction, resulting in reduced molecular weight and high levels of contamination from salts and bio-fouling, which can decrease recycling output efficiency by over 30%.

## Product Portfolio and Chemical Composition Specifications

To process these challenging raw inputs, the Seaqual Initiative has developed a diverse product matrix tailored for different industrial applications. The flagship product, SEAQUAL® YARN, is a high-quality recycled polyester yarn available in both continuous filament and staple fiber formats. To maintain the strict physical performance standards required by modern textile machinery, the yarn is manufactured as a blend of 10% Upcycled Marine Plastic (derived from marine litter) and 90% post-consumer polyethylene terephthalate (rPET) from terrestrial sources, such as land-based bottle collection systems.

This 10:90 blending ratio is a deliberate engineering constraint. Because the marine polymer has been weakened by environmental weathering, it cannot dominate the blend without sacrificing the yarn's performance characteristics—such as strength, consistency, and spinability—that manufacturers require. By blending the degraded marine polymer with 90% high-quality terrestrial rPET, the initiative restores structural integrity while maintaining a high-performance recycled product.

|**Product Name**|**Marine Content & Source**|**Terrestrial/Alternative Content**|**Polymer Base**|**Spliced Formats**|**Typical Applications**|
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|**SEAQUAL® YARN**|10% Upcycled Marine Plastic (coastal/estuary PET bottles)|90% Land-based post-consumer rPET|Polyester (PET)|Continuous filament & staple fiber|Fashion apparel, sportswear, activewear, home upholstery|
|**SEAQUAL® FIBER**|20% Upcycled Marine Plastic (beach & river bottles)|80% Land-based post-consumer rPET|Polyester (PET)|Staple fiber blends|Non-woven textiles, acoustic panels, fiberfill insulation, automotive carpets|
|**SEAQUAL® NYLON YARN**|100% Upcycled Marine Plastic (Mediterranean fishing nets)|0% Terrestrial Content|Polyamide 6 (PA6)|Extruded continuous filament|Performance swimwear, wetsuits, water-repellent sportswear|
|**SEAQUAL® MARINE POLYMER**|100% Upcycled Marine Plastic (ocean bottles/nets)|0% Terrestrial Content|PET, PP, or PA6|Extruded compound pellets|Injection molding, outdoor furniture, consumer goods, automotive trim|
|**SEAQUAL® YARN T2T**|10% Upcycled Marine Plastic (coastal/estuary PET bottles)|90% Chemically recycled post-consumer textiles|Polyester (PET)|Melt-spun continuous filament|Closed-loop circular sportswear, performance jerseys, uniform wear|

The physical properties of the finished polyester yarns are practically identical to those of virgin polyester fiber, exhibiting equal tensile strength, durability, and hand-feel. For specific applications, such as Burlington's Restora collection, specialized yarn configurations have been engineered to contain between 3% and 7% ocean plastics, with the remaining 93% to 95% comprised of high-purity recycled land PET, showing that the exact blend ratio can be customized to meet the strict aesthetic requirements of outdoor performance gear.

## Supply Chain Verticality, Spinners, and Manufacturing Infrastructure

The Seaqual Initiative operates a highly vertically integrated supply chain, managing the lifecycle of recovered waste from marine retrieval to finished textile materials. This structural verticality is anchored by Seaqual's primary industrial partners and shareholders, Antex and VICA. Both companies are established spinning manufacturers with industrial roots dating back to 1969.

Antex, operating facilities in Spain, Brazil, and Mexico, specializes in the extrusion of continuous filament polyester and polyamide yarns. VICA focuses on the processing of short staple fibers. By maintaining direct ownership and close partnerships with these manufacturing facilities, Seaqual exercises tight control over quality, material integrity, and chain of custody. This level of integration prevents supply chain fragmentation and mitigates the logistical challenges of handling volatile and contaminated raw inputs.

```

   └─ Coastal sweep and harbor retrieval across global hubs [9, 12]

   └─ On-site shredding into "dirty flakes" 

   └─ Washing at 80°C in Spain to remove salts and bio-contaminants 
[ Compounding & Pelletization ]
   └─ Extruding clean flakes into 100% SEAQUAL® Marine Polymer pellets 

   └─ Spinning with land-based rPET at Antex and VICA (Spain and Mexico) 

   └─ Embedding DNA tracers and logging transaction data via AWARE 
```

Traceability is guaranteed through a dual-mechanism verification system. First, Seaqual material is embedded with a proprietary "DNA tracer," a chemical biomarker added during the extrusion phase that allows lab analysis to verify the authentic presence of Upcycled Marine Plastic in finished fabrics. Second, the initiative utilizes the AWARE Integrity System, a digital chain-of-custody platform that records transaction data and environmental impact metrics on a public blockchain. This transparent governance framework ensures compliance with the Global Recycled Standard (GRS) and Oeko-Tex Standard 100, providing brands with audit-ready documentation to satisfy rigorous ESG disclosure requirements.

## Discard Destinies: Downcycling, Chemical Recycling, and Brand-Led Take-Back Programs

A critical challenge for sustainable textiles is the end-of-life phase, as traditional garment disposal often leads to landfilling or incineration due to the complexity of blended fibers, chemical finishes, and performance coatings. For standard products made with polyester-based SEAQUAL® YARN, mechanical recycling routes face thermodynamic limits; repeated mechanical melting shortens the polymer chains, progressively downcycling the material into lower-value applications like non-woven insulation, cushion filling, or industrial strapping.

To address these end-of-life limitations, the Seaqual Initiative has expanded into advanced chemical recycling. Seaqual has successfully implemented commercial runs of chemical depolymerization. This process breaks down post-consumer polyester textiles at a molecular level into their basic chemical monomers, purifying the material of dyes, finishes, and blending contaminants. The monomers are then repolymerized into virgin-equivalent polyester.

This chemical recycling technology has enabled the launch of SEAQUAL® YARN T2T (Textile-to-Textile). This next-generation yarn maintains the standard 10% Upcycled Marine Plastic content, but replaces the 90% terrestrial post-consumer bottle-reclaimed PET with 90% chemically recycled textile waste. This shift is highly significant for the circular economy, as it allows post-consumer garments to be recycled back into high-performance fibers indefinitely, while keeping terrestrial PET bottles within closed-loop bottle-to-bottle packaging systems.

In addition to this systemic chemical pathway, several licensed brand partners have implemented proprietary take-back and circular design initiatives to manage end-of-life products:

- **Isomi (Knit One Chair):** This furniture system features a modular design that eliminates the use of foam adhesives and heavy composites, replacing traditional upholstery with a 3D-knitted sleeve made of SEAQUAL® YARN. The sleeve is engineered for easy removal and mechanical disassembly, enabling straightforward recycling of both the textile cover and the metal frame at end-of-life.
- **Duvaltex (Ocean Waste Clean Impact Textiles):** Developed in collaboration with Steelcase, these commercial office upholstery fabrics are designed to be 100% recyclable, utilizing zero-waste knitting technologies that match the performance of virgin synthetics while facilitating clean material reclamation.
- **SMART Acoustic Panel Series:** Fabricated from SEAQUAL® FIBER to achieve Sound Absorber Class A performance under EN ISO 11654, these panels contain at least 65% recycled PET. The manufacturing brand provides a free-of-charge take-back and disposal service, ensuring the acoustic cores are directly recovered and reintegrated into new industrial fiber lines at the end of their useful lifecycle.
- **Burlington (Restora Collection):** Burlington has integrated Seaqual materials with Teijin's Eco Circle™ closed-loop recycling system. This chemical recycling infrastructure accepts returned garments, strips out elastomeric additives and colorants, and purifies the polyester back to its original performance levels.
- **Wetheknot (Circular Platform):** This accessory and apparel brand has established a circular take-back framework that resells pre-owned items, and offers repair and upcycling services to extend the operational lifetime of products before final recycling.

## Comparative Life-Cycle Assessment and Environmental Footprint Analysis

To quantify the environmental benefits of upcycling marine debris, Antex conducted a comparative cradle-to-gate Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) in accordance with ISO 14040 and ISO 14044 standards. The study used Simapro v9.1 software to evaluate the environmental footprint of producing 1 kg (functional unit) of various polyester yarns. The boundaries of the cradle-to-gate assessment encompassed raw material procurement, sorting, mechanical hot-washing, pelletization, and the spinning and texturing processes at the manufacturing plant.

|**Environmental Indicator (per 1 kg Yarn)**|**Virgin PET Yarn**|**Ynviron (Land-based rPET)**|**SEAQUAL® YARN (10% Marine / 90% Land rPET)**|**Yarnback (Pre-consumer rPET)**|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**Global Warming Potential** ($kg\ CO_2\ eq$)|6.09|3.84|3.84|2.87|
|**Water Consumption** ($m^3$)|$8.37 \times 10^{-2}$|$6.22 \times 10^{-2}$|$6.22 \times 10^{-2}$|$5.48 \times 10^{-2}$|
|**Total Energy Consumption** ($MJ$)|164.15|98.58|98.58|63.56|

The LCA data reveals that the environmental profile of SEAQUAL® YARN is identical to that of standard post-consumer recycled polyester (such as Ynviron). Both post-consumer recycled yarns achieve significant resource savings compared to virgin PET. Specifically, the transition from virgin polyester to SEAQUAL® YARN yields a 37% reduction in global warming potential, a 34% reduction in water consumption, and a 40% reduction in total electricity and energy demand throughout the manufacturing lifecycle.

These resource savings are further enhanced when brands combine the recycled yarn with advanced finishing techniques. For instance, utilizing mass solution dyeing (dope dyeing) in the spinning process bypasses the water-intensive package-dyeing stage, reducing water consumption and energy demand even further, while also preventing the release of microplastics during subsequent wet processing.

While pre-consumer recycled yarns (such as Yarnback) exhibit slightly higher reductions in greenhouse gas emissions (42%) and water usage (49%) due to the absence of complex sorting and cleaning steps, SEAQUAL® YARN provides a vital "curative" ecological service. By establishing a commercial value for highly degraded marine plastics, the initiative funds expensive cleanup operations that would otherwise be economically unviable. This helps prevent the degradation of macro-plastics into persistent microplastics and protects fragile marine ecosystems.

## Strategic Industry Syntheses and Actionable Recommendations

The commercial growth of the Seaqual Initiative highlights several key opportunities and operational strategies for brands seeking to integrate these circular materials:

- **Designing for Disassembly:** Brands should prioritize monomaterial construction when utilizing SEAQUAL® YARN to ensure the resulting products can be easily recycled at end-of-life. Avoiding complex fiber blends (such as polyester-cotton or polyester-elastane) and eliminating toxic adhesives or permanent metallic trims simplifies mechanical sorting and ensures the fabrics can enter chemical depolymerization streams without degrading the recycling output.
- **Leveraging the Dual Value-Chain Story:** Because Seaqual materials carry both an industrial performance certification (GRS, Oeko-Tex) and a compelling social narrative, marketing departments can position these products to drive consumer engagement. Highlighting the direct connection to coastal cleanup crews and fishing cooperatives allows brands to justify premium price points while actively funding global ocean regeneration.
- **Mitigating Microplastic Shedding:** While upcycling marine plastic is a vital curative solution, synthetic fabrics can still release microplastics during consumer washing. Fabric designers should recommend cold-water washing, mild detergents, or the use of specialized washbags. Alternatively, utilizing SEAQUAL® YARN in applications with low wash-frequencies—such as footwear uppers, upholstery, non-woven insulation, or acoustic panels—minimizes microplastic release while maximizing the material's operational lifetime.
- **Participating in Take-Back Infrastructure:** Large-scale commercial consumers of Seaqual textiles should establish direct partnerships with specialized chemical recyclers or implement brand-led take-back programs. By setting up returned-garment collection hubs, brands can secure their own feedstock for next-generation Textile-to-Textile (T2T) yarn production, helping to insulate their supply chains from future regulatory penalties and raw-material price volatility.
