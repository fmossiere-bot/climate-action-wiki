## Composting as a solution
[[Composting - Project Drawdown 57]]

Composting provides many advantages:
- Composting reduces waste sent to landfills which is often incinerated and generates carbon emissions.
- Reducing landfill waste can reduce air pollution and associated respiratory illnesses such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, asthma, and pneumonia.
- When used for gardening or farming, compost reduce the need of fertilizers and pesticides in soils and helps improve soil health and thereby increase agricultural yields.
- Compost retains water, prevents erosion, reduces runoff, establishes vegetation, and improves downstream water quality by retaining pollutants like heavy metals, nitrogen, phosphorus, oil, grease, fuels, herbicides, and pesticides.

It is already quite high in most developed nations like the EU (57%). 
This is mostly achieved thanks to community composters, and better waste separation at industry, company and individual levels.

> Starting with the brown bin – which, if you are disposing of your rubbish correctly, should contain only cooked and raw food, light garden waste and compostable packaging. “The contents of the brown bins go for anerobic digestion or industrial composting and the waste management companies pay for it to be taken,” explains Crinion. There are seven private industrial composting facilities in Ireland licensed by local authorities or the EPA and the Department of Agriculture. The compost made at these sites is sold principally as peat replacement for landscaping, agriculture and horticulture. Source: https://www.irishtimes.com/life-and-style/where-does-the-waste-from-your-bins-end-up-1.4699268 

- About 800,000 householders throughout Ireland have a compostable waste bin (usually a brown bin) from a total of 1.3 million householders availing of kerbside waste collection services.
- The contents of about 10 per cent of brown bins are sent to anerobic digestors in Ireland and the UK where methane gas is extracted and turned into electricity fed into the grid
	- Overall though Seems this number is much higher in Ireland: https://www.epa.ie/our-services/monitoring--assessment/waste/national-waste-statistics/composting--anaerobic/ . Close to 50% is sent for AD, but majority is from Municipalities waste and agriculture. 
- The residual material – known as digestate – is used by farmers to spread on their land as an organic alternative to nitrogen fertilisers thus reducing the amount of greenhouse gases emitted into the atmosphere by this source.

## CO2-e impact of HouseHold food Waste

Source: ChatGPT | [document](https://docs.google.com/document/d/11c898lggdqgD0AGtpfXElg_vb1lbh_CIJtMm4VdKDfM/edit?tab=t.0)

Here’s a clear way to size this for an Irish household—and the headline numbers most people use.

### 1) How much food waste to assume

- Ireland’s EPA estimates **~162 kg of food waste per person per year (2023)**. ([epa.ie](https://www.epa.ie/our-services/monitoring--assessment/waste/national-waste-statistics/food/?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Food Waste Statistics | Environmental Protection Agency - EPA.ie"))
    
- Average Irish household size is **~2.74 people** → **~444 kg food waste per household per year** (162×2.74≈443.9). ([CSO](https://www.cso.ie/en/releasesandpublications/ep/p-cpsr/censusofpopulation2022-summaryresults/householdsizeandmaritalstatus/?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Household Size and Marital Status Census of Population 2022 - CSO"))
    

### 2) Climate impact of the wasted food itself (production → retail → home)

A good mixed-diet average for embodied emissions is **~3.07 kg CO₂e per kg of food** (midpoint from FLW Protocol guidance based on Poore & Nemecek factors; typical ranges you’ll also see are ~2.5–3.9). ([Food Loss and Waste Protocol](https://flwprotocol.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/ConnectingFLWGHG-Emissions_GuidanceForCompanies.pdf?utm_source=chatgpt.com "[PDF] Connecting Food Loss and Waste to Greenhouse Gas Emissions:"), [americaspg.com](https://americaspg.com/article/pdf/1825?utm_source=chatgpt.com "[PDF] The Economic Impacts of Food Waste Carbon Footprint on climate ..."), [wrap.ngo](https://www.wrap.ngo/resources/report/household-food-and-drink-waste-uk-2022?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Household Food and Drink Waste in the UK 2022 - WRAP"))

- **Annual embodied impact** ≈ 444 kg × 3.07 ≈ **1.36 t CO₂e per household**.  
    (Range using 2.5–3.9 gives roughly **1.11–1.73 t CO₂e/yr**.) ([Food Loss and Waste Protocol](https://flwprotocol.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/ConnectingFLWGHG-Emissions_GuidanceForCompanies.pdf?utm_source=chatgpt.com "[PDF] Connecting Food Loss and Waste to Greenhouse Gas Emissions:"), [americaspg.com](https://americaspg.com/article/pdf/1825?utm_source=chatgpt.com "[PDF] The Economic Impacts of Food Waste Carbon Footprint on climate ..."), [wrap.ngo](https://www.wrap.ngo/resources/report/household-food-and-drink-waste-uk-2022?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Household Food and Drink Waste in the UK 2022 - WRAP"))
    

### 3) Extra impact from _not composting_ (i.e., putting it in the residual bin)

For Ireland, residual household waste is largely sent to **energy-from-waste (incineration) or landfill** (2022: ~43% EfW, ~15% landfill overall), but the **worst-case** penalty is landfill because of methane. ([epa.ie](https://www.epa.ie/our-services/monitoring--assessment/waste/national-waste-statistics/municipal/?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Municipal waste statistics for Ireland - EPA.ie"))

UK/WRAP CarbonWARM/DEFRA factors are widely used proxies for disposal pathways:

- **Landfill (food+garden)**: **~592 kg CO₂e per tonne**
    
- **Composting (food+garden)**: **~54 kg CO₂e per tonne**  
    → **Delta landfill vs composting ≈ 538 kg CO₂e per tonne = 0.538 kg per kg**. ([assets.publishing.service.gov.uk](https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/media/63974500e90e077c329444f0/Statistics_on_carbon_emmisions_Waste_Households_England_v8_2018.pdf?utm_source=chatgpt.com "[PDF] Experimental Statistics on the carbon impact of waste from ... - GOV.UK"), [MeasureUp](https://measure-up.org/mu-outcomes/waste-diversion-from-landfill/?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Waste Diversion from Landfill | Outcomes - MeasureUp"))
    

Apply that delta to the household’s food waste:

- **Annual penalty for not composting** ≈ 444 kg × 0.538 ≈ **0.24 t CO₂e per household**. ([assets.publishing.service.gov.uk](https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/media/63974500e90e077c329444f0/Statistics_on_carbon_emmisions_Waste_Households_England_v8_2018.pdf?utm_source=chatgpt.com "[PDF] Experimental Statistics on the carbon impact of waste from ... - GOV.UK"))
    

(If your residual waste goes to EfW rather than landfill, the penalty is **smaller** than 0.24 t; landfill is the conservative/worst-case assumption. Ireland still landfills a portion of municipal waste, so composting/AD remains important.) ([epa.ie](https://www.epa.ie/our-services/monitoring--assessment/waste/national-waste-statistics/municipal/?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Municipal waste statistics for Ireland - EPA.ie"))

### Bottom line per average household (Ireland)

- **Wasting the food at all (embodied emissions): ~1.36 t CO₂e/yr**
- **Extra for not composting (vs composting/AD): ~0.24 t CO₂e/yr**
- **Total climate opportunity** if you both _prevent the waste_ and _compost what remains_: **~1.6 t CO₂e per year per household** (central estimate). ([epa.ie](https://www.epa.ie/our-services/monitoring--assessment/waste/national-waste-statistics/food/?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Food Waste Statistics | Environmental Protection Agency - EPA.ie"), [CSO](https://www.cso.ie/en/releasesandpublications/ep/p-cpsr/censusofpopulation2022-summaryresults/householdsizeandmaritalstatus/?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Household Size and Marital Status Census of Population 2022 - CSO"), [Food Loss and Waste Protocol](https://flwprotocol.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/ConnectingFLWGHG-Emissions_GuidanceForCompanies.pdf?utm_source=chatgpt.com "[PDF] Connecting Food Loss and Waste to Greenhouse Gas Emissions:"), [assets.publishing.service.gov.uk](https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/media/63974500e90e077c329444f0/Statistics_on_carbon_emmisions_Waste_Households_England_v8_2018.pdf?utm_source=chatgpt.com "[PDF] Experimental Statistics on the carbon impact of waste from ... - GOV.UK"))

### Quick “per-kg” rules of thumb

- **Prevent 1 kg of food waste** → save **~3.07 kg CO₂e** (production → home). ([Food Loss and Waste Protocol](https://flwprotocol.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/ConnectingFLWGHG-Emissions_GuidanceForCompanies.pdf?utm_source=chatgpt.com "[PDF] Connecting Food Loss and Waste to Greenhouse Gas Emissions:"))
- **If you still waste it, but compost instead of landfill** → avoid **~0.54 kg CO₂e per kg**. ([assets.publishing.service.gov.uk](https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/media/63974500e90e077c329444f0/Statistics_on_carbon_emmisions_Waste_Households_England_v8_2018.pdf?utm_source=chatgpt.com "[PDF] Experimental Statistics on the carbon impact of waste from ... - GOV.UK"))

### How to tailor to _your_ home

Use these simple formulas:

1. **Embodied impact** (t CO₂e/yr)  
    = (162 kg/person/yr × household size) × (EF_food, default 3.07 kg CO₂e/kg) ÷ 1,000. ([epa.ie](https://www.epa.ie/our-services/monitoring--assessment/waste/national-waste-statistics/food/?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Food Waste Statistics | Environmental Protection Agency - EPA.ie"), [Food Loss and Waste Protocol](https://flwprotocol.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/ConnectingFLWGHG-Emissions_GuidanceForCompanies.pdf?utm_source=chatgpt.com "[PDF] Connecting Food Loss and Waste to Greenhouse Gas Emissions:"))
    
2. **Not-composting penalty** (t CO₂e/yr, worst-case)  
    = (162 kg/person/yr × household size) × **0.538** ÷ 1,000. ([assets.publishing.service.gov.uk](https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/media/63974500e90e077c329444f0/Statistics_on_carbon_emmisions_Waste_Households_England_v8_2018.pdf?utm_source=chatgpt.com "[PDF] Experimental Statistics on the carbon impact of waste from ... - GOV.UK"))
