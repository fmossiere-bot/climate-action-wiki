#fashion 

There are many ways the Fashion industry is hurting the planet:
- Pesticides used to grow the natural fabrics like Cotton - reducing biodiversity
- Toxic Dyes pollute the waterways
- Fossil Fuel used to make synthetic fibres, often used to make cheap clothes, bought by armful and worn just a few times. These kind of clothes leaches micrplastics into the rivers and oceans with each wash
- Production Processes resiult in a lot of waste, with unused fabrics heading straight to scrap


> [!Here are some statistics about the environmental impacts of fashion:]
> - The fashion industry currently accounts for about 10% of global emissions and the industry is the second-largest consumer of water on the planet.[1](https://earth.org/fast-fashions-detrimental-effect-on-the-environment/)
> - Between 80 and 100 billion new clothing garments are produced globally every year.[2](https://theroundup.org/textile-waste-statistics/)
> - 87% of the materials and fibers used to make clothing will end up in either incinerators or landfills.[2](https://theroundup.org/textile-waste-statistics/)
> - Only 20% of discarded textiles are collected.[2](https://theroundup.org/textile-waste-statistics/)
> - Only 1% of clothes will get recycled into new garments.[2](https://theroundup.org/textile-waste-statistics/)
> - About 35% of the microplastics that enter the ocean come from washing  synthetic textiles.[1](https://earth.org/fast-fashions-detrimental-effect-on-the-environment/)]

##### Unspun
With the promess of local factories in local communities, 3D priniting holds a lot of hope. Imagine it could work for fashion? Instead of mass procuding miles away, you could produce woven fgarments on demand? Unspun has invetned a 3d Weaving technology for clothing
![[Screenshot 2024-09-24 at 12.17.31.png]]

- making clothes this way could cut multiple production steps, and save waste. 
###### In traditional processes, about 15% of fabric ends up on the cutting room floor. 

- It is time saving. Unspun Vage machine can weave 2 tubes of fabric in 10min for trousers
It will allow localisation, but also impact jobs in those countries who rely on them though? How do we make sure there is a fair transition there? 
Unspun teamed up with Walmart to work a pilot project taht aims to have 350 Vega machines waeving trousers for its shop by 2030

##### Solena
> Imagine wearing a raincoat made from fabric that is as soft as cashmere, but as waterproof as plastic? 

Solena uses copmuters to analyse and design proteins for the fashion industry to address the problem: 
" Natural fibre like cotton and silk tend to lack toughness and are intensive to produce, while synthetic fibres tend to rely on fossil fuel for producting and shed microplastics . "

He is aiming at making a whole new class of biodegradable, functional, protein based ones. 

###### In theory, AI could develop a protein with any charcteristics you muight want

##### Pact
Ethical reasons for avoinding leather are well-rehearsed, but the enviornmental factors is often overlooked. 
- It support a livestock industry, since skin is from goat, sheep and cows
- Traditional leather comprises 90% of collagen

Oval, part of Pact, use the same building blocks, but instead of using livestock, it is made from collagen collected from fish watste scales. 

###### Wherre conventional cow skin leather has a carbon footprint of 110 kg/m2, PACT footprint is about 3kg
