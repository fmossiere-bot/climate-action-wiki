## Here is the carbon footprint of an average day of emails, WhatsApps and more
Source: [https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2024/oct/31/concerned-about-your-data-use-here-is-the-carbon-footprint-of-an-average-day-of-emails-whatsapps-and-more](https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2024/oct/31/concerned-about-your-data-use-here-is-the-carbon-footprint-of-an-average-day-of-emails-whatsapps-and-more)


The ever-increasing global appetite for data is driving the rapid expansion of massive datacentres, sparking serious worries about their environmental costs. Just like oil, our digital dependency is proving to be detrimental to the planet. Every online action, from sending emails to streaming videos and engaging with social media, relies on these energy-hungry facilities, contributing to a substantial yet often unseen carbon footprint.

Calculating this impact is challenging due to a lack of transparency from tech companies, with estimates of the internet's energy usage ranging from 3.7% to 10% of the global total. The surge in AI adoption further intensifies these concerns, as AI-powered tasks and generative AI tools demand significantly more computational power and resources, including water for cooling.

While major tech corporations are investing in carbon offsets and exploring energy solutions like small nuclear reactors, these measures are often viewed as insufficient. Experts emphasize the need for individual action, promoting the concept of "digital sobriety." This involves being more conscious of our online habits, questioning the necessity of every digital interaction, and opting for lower-data alternatives where possible. By adopting a more mindful approach to our digital consumption, we can collectively strive to lessen the environmental burden of our increasingly data-driven lives.

Estimating how much data your daily activities use is an art not a science, but here are best estimates of how much you are gobbling up online.

- **Listening to a podcast:** [20-100MB an hour](https://podcastpontifications.com/episode/apple-podcasts-switch-to-streaming-will-decimate-downloads/#:~:text=Average%20Data%20Consumption%20per%20Hour,techniques%20employed%20by%20different%20platforms.)
- **Watching Netflix:** 3GB an hour at HD quality
- **Online shopping:** Consider the data size of any images you browse, which can be big, before even thinking of the environmental impact of your delivery
- **WhatsApp text message:** [1-5KB a message](https://www.airalo.com/blog/how-much-data-does-whatsapp-use?srsltid=AfmBOopDreq5v_Lg7jQWoAXtnkT-IKNXyLMijt9m4sJYWzNK3sk84Bjv), on average
- **WhatsApp voice call:** [400KB-1MB](https://www.airalo.com/blog/how-much-data-does-whatsapp-use?srsltid=AfmBOopDreq5v_Lg7jQWoAXtnkT-IKNXyLMijt9m4sJYWzNK3sk84Bjv) a minute
- **WhatsApp video call:** [2.5-15MB](https://www.airalo.com/blog/how-much-data-does-whatsapp-use?srsltid=AfmBOopDreq5v_Lg7jQWoAXtnkT-IKNXyLMijt9m4sJYWzNK3sk84Bjv) a minute
- **Average pre-AI Google search:** [500KB](https://medium.com/@DMSYAID/unlocking-the-mystery-how-much-data-does-google-search-use-dd21fb750c43#:~:text=Google%20Search%20Data%20Usage,-Now%2C%20let's%20take&text=On%20average%2C%20a%20simple%20text,data%20usage%20will%20increase%20significantly.) for a text-based search
- **Average post-AI Google search:** No one knows …
- **Sending an email:** Depends on the size of the message, but [about 75KB on average](https://mailmeteor.com/tools/email-size#:~:text=The%20average%20email%20size%20can,around%2075%20kilobytes%20\(KB\).)
- **Sending an email with photo attachment:** As above, plus the size of the attachment
- **Downloading an album on Spotify:** Depends on your audio quality, but around [72MB](https://www.androidauthority.com/spotify-data-usage-918265/) for an hour-long album
- **Playing a game of Fortnite:** [Between 45 and 100MB an hour](https://www.evdodepotusa.com/much-data-fortnite-download-use/#:~:text=On%20Epic%20Games%20forums%2C%20gamers,1%20GB%20of%20mobile%20data.)
