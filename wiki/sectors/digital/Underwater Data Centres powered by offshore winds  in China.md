# Underwater Data Centre — China 2026

## What is it

In May 2026, China switched on the world's first commercial **underwater data centre (UDC)**, located off the coast of Shanghai. Built by a subsidiary of China Communications Construction and powered primarily by offshore wind, it represents a genuinely novel approach to the land, water, and energy problems that conventional data centres create.

The facility houses 192 server racks across four levels, sits on the seabed in the Lin-gang Special Area, and uses the surrounding ocean as a natural heat sink — eliminating the need for freshwater cooling entirely.

## Why it matters

Data centres are one of the fastest-growing consumers of electricity and water. Most of the world's 8,808+ data centres are built on land, in locations chosen for connectivity and power access rather than environmental efficiency. The UDC concept challenges those assumptions directly. By going underwater, this facility cuts land use by over 90%, removes freshwater from the equation, and taps offshore wind for most of its power — all at once.

It also arrives at a moment when the data centre industry in the UK and Ireland is turning to fossil gas because grids cannot supply power fast enough. The UDC model points to a different path.

## How it works

The facility uses a **circulating copper-pipe heat exchange system** that transfers heat from the servers into the surrounding seawater without mixing the two. This reportedly reduces electricity consumption for cooling by 22.8% compared with conventional systems. Professor Li Zhen from Tsinghua University estimates that cooling-related electricity for an equivalent-scale underwater facility falls to roughly one-tenth that of a land-based centre.

Offshore wind farms generate an estimated **95% of the electricity** needed to run the facility. The centre is currently operating at 2.3 MW but has a planned capacity of 24 MW — enough to power around 20,000 homes. This built-in headroom is designed to accommodate future hardware upgrades rather than requiring new infrastructure.

## What remains uncertain

The UDC concept has not yet been tested at full commercial scale. Two key questions remain open:

1. **Ecological impact** — Continuously releasing heat into a local marine environment through the heat exchange system could affect water temperature and marine life. Research on this is at an early stage.
2. **Maintenance and longevity** — Accessing underwater infrastructure for repairs is fundamentally harder than on land. How these facilities will hold up over years of operation is still unknown.

## Key facts

- World's first commercial underwater data centre; located off Shanghai, operational from May 2026.
- Uses **seawater cooling** — no freshwater required.
- Land use reduced by **more than 90%** vs above-ground centres.
- Cooling electricity consumption reduced by **22.8%** via copper-pipe heat exchange.
- **95% of power from offshore wind**.
- Current capacity: **2.3 MW**; planned capacity: **24 MW**.
- Potential global saving if scaled: **50 billion kWh/year** in cooling electricity.
- Open questions remain on marine ecological effects. ([New Atlas, 2026](https://newatlas.com/energy/china-underwater-data-center-opens/))
