### Environmental Impact
*==Source: ChatGPT==*

The tyre industry has a significant impact on the environment, both through the manufacturing process and the disposal of used tires. Here are some key points:

1. **Resource Consumption**: The production of tires requires large amounts of raw materials, including rubber (both natural and synthetic), steel, and petroleum-based products. The extraction and processing of these materials contribute to environmental degradation, including deforestation, habitat destruction, and pollution.
2. **Energy Use and Emissions**: Tire manufacturing is energy-intensive and contributes to greenhouse gas emissions. The industry relies heavily on fossil fuels, both in the production process and in the supply chain.
3. **Pollution**: The manufacturing process can lead to the release of various pollutants, including volatile organic compounds (VOCs), particulate matter, and other toxic substances. These pollutants can affect air and water quality and pose risks to human health and the environment
4. **Waste and Disposal Issues**: Disposal of used tires is a major environmental challenge. Tires are non-biodegradable and can take up significant space in landfills. When not disposed of properly, they can become breeding grounds for mosquitoes and other pests. Additionally, illegal dumping of tires can lead to soil and water contamination.
5. **Tire Burning**: Burning old tires for energy or disposal releases toxic chemicals into the air, contributing to air pollution and health risks.
    
6. **Microplastics and Particulate Emissions**: Tires contribute to microplastic pollution through tire wear on roads. These particles can be transported by wind and water, contaminating ecosystems and entering the food chain.
    
7. **Water Runoff**: Runoff from roads can carry harmful chemicals from tires into waterways, affecting aquatic life and water quality.
    
8. **Innovation and Sustainability Efforts**: In response to these environmental challenges, the tire industry is exploring more sustainable practices, including the development of eco-friendly materials, recycling initiatives, and efforts to reduce emissions in the production process.
    

Overall, while tires are essential for modern transportation, the industry faces significant environmental challenges and is under increasing pressure to adopt more sustainable practices.

### CO2-e Impact
*==Source: ChatGPT==*

Quantifying the exact impact of the tire industry on CO2 emissions is complex due to several factors, including the diversity of manufacturing processes, the geographical spread of production facilities, and differences in regulatory environments. However, some general points can be made:

1. **Manufacturing Emissions**: The CO2 emissions from the manufacturing of tires are significant, mainly due to the energy-intensive nature of the production process. This includes emissions from the extraction and processing of raw materials, as well as the manufacturing process itself.
    
2. **Raw Material Extraction**: The extraction of raw materials like rubber, steel, and petrochemicals for tire production involves significant CO2 emissions. For instance, the deforestation for natural rubber plantations releases CO2 stored in trees.
    
3. **Transport Emissions**: The global nature of the tire supply chain means transportation (of raw materials, intermediate products, and finished tires) contributes to CO2 emissions.
    
4. **End-of-Life Emissions**: The disposal of tires, especially through methods like burning, releases CO2 and other greenhouse gases.
    
5. **Lifecycle Emissions**: Tires contribute indirectly to CO2 emissions through their impact on vehicle fuel efficiency. Heavier or less efficient tires can increase a vehicle's fuel consumption, thereby increasing its CO2 emissions.
    
6. **Innovations to Reduce CO2**: The tire industry is actively working on reducing its carbon footprint through innovations in manufacturing, material science, and recycling.
    

To get specific numbers regarding the CO2 emissions of the tire industry, one would need data from industry reports, environmental impact studies, or specific company disclosures. However, these general points highlight that while the tire industry is a significant contributor to CO2 emissions, efforts are being made to mitigate this impact.
