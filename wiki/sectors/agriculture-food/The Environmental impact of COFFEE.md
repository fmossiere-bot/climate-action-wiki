## Impact on Water resources

Source: https://www.eraofwe.com/coffee-lab/en/articles/the-importance-of-water-in-coffee-production
Research: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/365870511_Estimating_the_impact_on_water_scarcity_due_to_coffee_production_trade_and_consumption_worldwide_and_a_focus_on_EU

> Coffee trees are mainly rain-fed and only partly irrigated. However, the increasing global coffee demand led to higher consumption of freshwater, which can exacerbate the stressed condition of already stressed water basins. This study quantifies the impact of global coffee consumption on water scarcity, considering the larger system made of producer and consumer countries.

Source Amount of water: https://www.theguardian.com/sustainable-business/gallery/how-much-water-to-make-food-drink
Source: https://www.climatecouncil.org.au/trouble-is-brewing/


Water is a vital resource in the coffee production process, from cultivation and harvesting to processing and brewing. However, the coffee industry faces significant challenges in managing water consumption and ensuring sustainable water use practices. This article will explore the role of water in coffee production, the environmental implications of water consumption, and potential strategies for improving water efficiency and sustainability in the coffee industry.

The high water consumption associated with coffee production can have significant environmental consequences, particularly in water-scarce regions. Some key concerns include:

1. Water scarcity: The excessive use of water in coffee production can deplete local water resources, leading to water scarcity for other agricultural, industrial, and domestic uses.
2. Water pollution: The improper application of agrochemicals and wastewater from coffee processing can contaminate local water sources, posing risks to ecosystems, wildlife, and human health.
3. Habitat degradation: Excessive water extraction from rivers and other natural water sources can lead to habitat degradation and negatively impact aquatic ecosystems.

> [!Did you know?]
> How much water goes into making your 1 cup of coffee? 
> Answer: Around 18,900 litres of water are needed to produce 1kg of coffee beans. To put that into perspective, a standard cup of coffee (125ml) has a water footprint of 130 litres

> [!Capsule vs Filter coffee]
> Compared to traditional filter coffee, drinking a capsule filter coffee (280 ml) saves between 11 and 13 grams of coffee. Producing 11 grams of [Arabica coffee in Brazil](https://doi.org/10.1002/geo2.96) emits about [59 grams of CO2e](https://ecoinvent.org/the-ecoinvent-database/) (CO2 equivalent). This value is much higher than the 27 grams of CO2e emitted for manufacturing of coffee capsules and sending the generated waste to a landfill. These figures give an idea of the importance of avoiding overusing and wasting coffee.

### What about Moka Pot coffee? 
Source: https://www.irishexaminer.com/lifestyle/outdoors/arid-41075792.html

According to this article and the research they referred to, Moka Pot would have significantly less impact than capsule coffee

> In 2021, a team of Italian researchers found that using a Moka pot, a stovetop coffee maker popular in Europe and Latin America, can produce significantly fewer greenhouse gasses than a capsule. (The study said that because of the extra packaging and post-consumer waste disposal, “the preparation of a cup of coffee with a coffee with a coffee pod or capsule machine would result in extra emissions of 27.6 or 12.6g CO2e with respect to those emitted with an induction Moka pot, respectively”.) Even the Canadian team’s analysis found that instant coffee had fewer emissions.

## Conclusion

- All studies seem consistent with the fact that POD./Capsule have the less impact. The reason for that is the amount of coffee (reduce overuse) and the energy used to prepare the coffee. But the amin issue is that people drink mmore because it is quick and convenient, which shift the impact. It is also much more expensive. 
- Moka pot seem to have less emissions that capsule as confirmed in some study from the irish Examiner article
- They have been many study, and all agree that the overuse of coffee (especially in coffee filter) and the main use for the different impact. The origin of coffee also makes a difference as coffee production is the biggest impact. In the case of Nestle it is 40%, but it can vary from 40-80%
- In other words, reducing your consumption of coffee is the most important. 
- Pod/capsule are not as bad as thought.
