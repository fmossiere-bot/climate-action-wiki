### Carbon Footprint impact
###### A kilo of rice can cause more emissions than burning a litre of diesel

The 2 main source of GHG from Rice farming are: 
- **Methane (CH₄):** Flooded rice paddies create anaerobic conditions that foster methane-producing microbes. Rice cultivation contributes approximately 10% of global agricultural methane emissions .​[FoodPrint](https://foodprint.org/blog/environmental-impacts-of-rice-production/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
- **Nitrous Oxide (N₂O):** Intermittent flooding, a method used to reduce methane emissions, can inadvertently increase nitrous oxide emissions, a greenhouse gas with a global warming potential nearly 300 times that of CO₂ .​

### Other Impact  about Rice Farming


- Rice is 20% of the world's food energy for just 3.5% of its Carbon Footprint. 
#### **Water Consumption**

Rice farming is water-intensive, accounting for nearly 30% of the world's freshwater usage. This high demand exacerbates water scarcity issues, especially in regions already facing water stress .​[Home | Freerice](https://freerice.com/blog/heres-why-sustainable-rice-cultivation-better-environment?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

#### **Soil and Air Quality**

- **Soil Degradation:** Continuous flooding can lead to soil nutrient depletion and reduced fertility over time.​
- **Air Pollution:** Post-harvest burning of rice straw releases pollutants, contributing to air quality deterioration and health issues .​[caes.ucdavis.edu](https://caes.ucdavis.edu/news/birds-and-fish-flooded-rice-fields-promoting-biodiversity-and-climate-protection?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

### There are ways to reduce Methane Production in Rice farming
See [[6 Ways to Reduce Methane from Agriculture#Curbing Methane from Rice]] 

One of the biggest problem of rice production is the excess water, and the excess use of fertiliser. 

> It is possible to grow rice without flooding the fieldand therfore cutting the methane emissions. Howerer, it's harder work, adn may require more fertiliser. 

To address these environmental concerns, several sustainable practices are being adopted:

- **Alternate Wetting and Drying (AWD):** This irrigation technique reduces methane emissions by up to 90% and conserves water without compromising yields .​[AP News+2FoodPrint+2arXiv+2](https://foodprint.org/blog/environmental-impacts-of-rice-production/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
    
- **Integrated Rice-Fish Farming:** Introducing fish into rice paddies can naturally control pests and reduce methane emissions by altering microbial communities .​[WIRED](https://www.wired.com/story/tiny-hungry-fish-fix-rice-global-warming-problem?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
    
- **Improved Fertilizer Management:** Precision application of fertilizers minimizes nitrous oxide emissions and prevents nutrient runoff.​
    
- **Straw Management:** Instead of burning, rice straw can be repurposed for livestock feed or mushroom cultivation, reducing air pollution and adding economic value
